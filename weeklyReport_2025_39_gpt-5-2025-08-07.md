# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

Key Takeaway (Sep 22–28, 2025): Spooky-season indie collabs dominated discourse, led by Fantasy Cosmetica x BattyBean. Creators leaned hard into witchy/goth aesthetics, complex shimmers, and red/purple/teal eye looks while expressing renewed skepticism toward mainstream complexion relaunches.

## 1. Overview

- The week felt like a coordinated indie push into Halloween: multiple collabs, heavy occult packaging, and maximalist, sparkle-rich eye looks. Palette content crowded feeds with live swatches, two-look demos, and atmospheric teasers.
- Sentiment clustered strongly positive around indie eyeshadow quality (pigmented mattes + multidimensional shimmers), with specific praise for red mattes that don’t feel dry or chalky.
- Value talk centered on “performance-per-dollar” in indie palettes versus mainstream; codes often excluded on collabs, yet creators still signaled “worth it” based on formula and artistry.
- Texture talk favored toppers/multichromes and graphic liner techniques to make reds look intentional. Glitter/adhesive primers were positioned as essential complements.
- Fatigue/skepticism surfaced around mainstream complexion (shade neutrality issues; thick finishes) and some lower-quality brow SKUs; creators preferred blendable stick contour/bronzer formats and comfort-matte lips.

Top mentioned indie products and sentiment:
- Fantasy Cosmetica x BattyBean “The Witch’s Familiar” palette ($49.99) — Strong positive. Cross-creator praise (BattyBean, Heather Austin, Rachel Palmieri, Angelica Nyqvist). Context: standout reds/purples/teals; sparkly duos/multichromes (Altar, Oracle, Moonlight). Magnetic pans; witchy/tarot packaging. Hype peak at launch-minus-3 days; codes excluded (still high intent).
- Lethal Cosmetics x The Conjuring — Positive buzz. Sponsored reveal (Angelica), additional coverage and a short highlighting the Music Box peach highlighter. Context: full themed collection (palette, highlighter, liners, primer, powder, nails), US warehouse launch reduces US buyer friction.

- Glamnetic “Red Bottom” press-on nails — Positive. Visual ASMR demo emphasized luxury-coded detail and Halloween synergy; nails served as a fashion accessory extension for dark glam looks.

Repeat notes and patterns:
- “Lots of creators testing Fantasy Cosmetica shimmers” — emphasis on complexity and wearable impact; FC shimmers becoming a default indie benchmark.
- “Toppers as a category” — Blend Bunny Efflorescence and FC’s sparkly shades drove a topper renaissance.
- “Red eye concern solved” — creators reiterated red/pink shadow needs grounding (colored or black waterline, precise bases) to avoid “sick” look; lining products (Rituel de Fille Blood, ColourPop red liner, NYX Glitter Primer) cited as must-haves.
- “Complexion caution” — Charlotte Tilbury’s new matte foundation drew mixed feedback (thick, off-neutral undertone); Morphe Nano Brow Pencil received negative marks for waxy snapping. MUFE HD Skin Concealer received positive utility praise.

## 3. Category Trends

- Eyeshadow palettes: Highest momentum. Collab-led, with jewel tones (reds/purples/teals), dark glamour, and heavy shimmer payoff. Seasonal tie-in to Halloween/witchy narratives is explicit.
- Toppers/multichromes: Strong uplift. Reframed as essential for high-impact looks; used as face highlights in some cases (e.g., FC Moonlight).
- Eyeliners/bases: Red and black liners, plus sticky/glitter primers, positioned as functional necessities for intentional red looks and shimmer longevity.
- Face: Cream/stick contour/bronzer formats praised for blendability and tone (Lunar Beauty, Morphe Shapelifter). Traditional “full-coverage matte” foundations faced skepticism; dewy/blurred blush textures still in rotation.
- Lips: Comfort-matte/soft-matte lipsticks and brown-leaning nudes paired with dramatic eyes; frequent lip combo shorts sustain low-effort, high-frequency engagement.
- Nails: Press-ons with luxe signifiers (“red bottom”) gained traction as quick seasonal statements, aligned with goth/alt styling.

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

Cross-creator themes (appearing 2+ creators):
- Witchy/Occult Aesthetic Surge: Tarot/black cat packaging (FC x BattyBean), horror tie-ins (Lethal x The Conjuring), and spooky props dominate visuals and copy.
- Shimmer Complexity as Differentiator: Duochrome/multichrome/toppers are the indie “value” hook; several creators positioned FC/Blend Bunny shimmers as quality leaders.
- “Intentional Red” Education: Red matte success stories plus liner techniques repeatedly demonstrated; red/berry metallics used to set graphic shapes.
- Collabs > Codes: Discount codes often excluded on collabs, yet purchase intent remains high; creators explicitly signal “codes won’t work” to preempt objections.
- Distribution Upgrades: Lethal’s US warehouse reduces tariffs/wait times; Fantasy Cosmetica flagged upcoming EU/Canada retail (Monolith, iFeelBeautiful) — cross-border friction dropping.
- Regional momentum: Western indie leads (US/EU/AU). EU indie (Lethal, Monolith) and AU indie (Glaminatrix) rise in visibility; minor K-beauty cameo (fwee blush pot), suggesting softening toward dewy cheek even in matte seasons.

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- High intent:
  - Fantasy Cosmetica x BattyBean: Scarcity-timed hype, repeated quality endorsements, creator-to-creator support narrative, collectible packaging.
  - Lethal x The Conjuring: Complete ecosystem (palette + accessories), US warehouse removes a key purchase barrier; peach highlighter singled out.
  - Toppers/multichromes: “Add-on” drivers; facilitate seasonal transformation without full palette purchase.
  - Red/black liners + glitter primers: Functional companions repeatedly recommended in tutorials.
  - Press-on nails with luxury cues: Immediate seasonal payoff; ASMR-style demos boost impulse buys.
- Mixed/negative dampeners:
  - Mainstream matte foundation relaunches (shade neutrality/texture complaints) lower conversion likelihood.
  - Fragile/waxy brow pencils (Morphe Nano) discourage trial.

- Shimmer/Toppers:
  - Multichrome/duochrome singles and topper palettes (own-brand or from indie suppliers with available wholesale). Position as “witchy eye builders” for October.
- Red/Purple/Teal Eye System:
  - Bundle: red/black gel liners, sticky/glitter primer (e.g., NYX-type), rich red/purple mattes, and a high-impact shimmer. Market as “Intentional Red Eye Kit.”
- Collab Adjacent:
  - If FC collab is exclusive, carry Fantasy Cosmetica’s non-collab items (brushes, other palettes) and build “Witch’s Familiar Companion Bundles” to monetize traffic.
  - If possible, secure Lethal Cosmetics (US warehouse = simpler logistics). Prioritize Music Box Highlighter, stamp liners, and themed nails/powders to ride the Conjuring wave.
- Press-on Nails:
  - Stock luxe-coded styles (matte black, hidden color undersides, stiletto). Create a “Dark Glam Nail Bar” section through Halloween.
- Stick Contour/Bronzer + Comfort-Matte Lips:
  - Curate blendable contour sticks (Lunar Beauty if available to wholesale or comparable indie alternatives).
  - Offer soft/comfort-matte lipsticks and brown-leaning liners that pair with bold eyes.

What to deprioritize/caution:
- Heavy matte foundations marketed as neutral but leaning yellow; vet undertone ranges or provide clear undertone guidance and shade quizzes.
- Waxy/fragile micro brow pencils; prioritize proven microfine textures that resist snapping.

Gaps to fill (identified by creators):
- True-neutral foundation tones (especially mid-range) with lighter textures.
- Reliable red waterline-safe liners and deep red/berry metallics (repeat emphasis shows demand).
- High-performance, affordable glitter/adhesive primers (frequent callouts; easy add-on).
