# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key Takeaway: Apr 13–19, 2026 saw chrome season collide with alt-grunge glam: orange/green iridescents and cool-toned grey palettes led the feed, while tutorial-style swatch reels and whimsical liner demos drove the strongest purchase chatter.

## 1. Overview

- The Vibe:
  - Heavy on quick-hit comparison swatches (“POV: you’re looking for X chrome”), face/arm swatches with mirror inserts, and mini tutorials (grunge eyes, whimsical fish-liner).
  - Alt/goth aesthetics stayed prominent (red velvet monochromes, black/grey/blue lips), balanced by springy pastel iridescents.
  - Multiple indie launch moments (Terra Moons Pastel Perihelion, BBB x Freya Yin & Yang, Melt Evergreens, Gourmande Girls Apparition, Kryptein Supernova).

- Engagement Temperature:
  - Hot: frequent “ordered,” code usage, shade-comparison questions, and tutorial requests.
  - Some friction: cruelty-free concerns under Sephora Collection ad; availability complaints (Wet n Wild chromes in-store, EU access).

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

- Top Mentioned Brands:
  - Terra Moons, Clionadh, Wet n Wild, Bella Beauté Bar, Unearthly, Trixie Cosmetics, Glisten Cosmetics, ColourPop, Melt, MOB Beauty, Dieverune, Maybelline, NYX.

- Viral Products:
  - Terra Moons Pastel Perihelion (6 chromes + 4 muted pastel mattes): “Ordered mine!!!”; strong interest in Nimbus, Between Stars, Rayleigh Scatter. EU availability questions.
  - Clionadh multi/duochromes (Green + Orange series): calls for smoothness comparisons (Hedge Maze vs Vineyard vs Niello); stacking code timing around anniversary sale boosted intent.
  - Wet n Wild Chameleon Chrome eyeshadows and Solar Ember highlighter: quality praise; “stores only have 2–3 shades” (stock gap); high value perception.
  - Trixie Cosmetics Lip Sync liners + Slix gloss sticks: “can’t put them down,” dry-lip relief; multiple shade combos gaining traction (Middle Child + Top Shelf; Babe Alert + Bottoms Up).
  - Kulfi staining lip liner (La La La): explicit sold‑out pain point; long-wear validation in weartest content.
  - MOB Beauty x Hanna The Void black gloss: “medium opacity black” validated for 90s grunge looks; appetite for alt-friendly glosses.
  - NYX x Simpsons Donut Highlight: “this is the only thing I want” / “I’ll need to get this” purchase cues.
  - Dieverune Dew Drops 1 & 2: “can’t do without,” face-swatches showcased utility as cheek/eye toppers.
  - Melt Evergreens mini stack + blush + liners: strong brand affinity, PR milestone posts, chartreuse “Sour Static” gasp moments.
  - Maybelline Lifter “lip oil in gel” with twist applicator: applicator delight; shade callouts (Iridescent blue, Strawberry Shake), primer questions.
  - ColourPop Super Shock Shadows: long-time “holy grail” and discovery excitement; cross-usage on lips/highlight.
  - Bella Beauté Bar x Freya Yin & Yang: heavy swatch demand, tone nuance on deeper skin addressed; “used your code” signals.

- The “Look” Breakdown:
  - Eyes:
    - Chrome toppers everywhere: oranges (peach→copper→pumpkin spice), greens (chartreuse→emerald→grungy grey-greens), and spring iridescents (mint→pink, pale blue→gold, yellow chromes).
    - Muted/navy/indigo mattes anchoring sparkle; bright lower-lash pops (electric blue, red mascara); graphic smoked wings; eyeliner used as eyeshadow.
  - Lips:
    - Grey glossy “nudes” (Glisten Veil/Gray Area; Kaleidos Stranger) and black gloss (MOB The Void) as alt staples.
    - Drugstore frosts for brown skin (CoverGirl Iced Mauve, Revlon Iced Mocha, Wet n Wild Cashmere) revived 90s finish with inclusive depth.
    - Cushiony gel/cream sticks (Trixie Slix; MBM Lip Sync creams) layered over cool liners; metallic/menacing blues (Unearthly Glacial Bite); stain-forward liners (Kulfi).
  - Cheeks/Highlight:
    - High-reflect iridescent toppers on face (Dieverune Dew Drops; TIRTIR Rose Gold Solar; Wet n Wild Solar Ember; NYX Donut).
    - Cream/soft-matte blush leaning pinky-nudes to muted mauves (Tower28 Malibu Cooler; Melt Chill; House of Hur Shade 5 with brush-pressure education).

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

- Stocking Recommendations:
  - Chrome Singles & Toppers:
    - Oranges: TM (Star Crossed, Firestorm, Callisto), Clionadh (Majesty, Monarch, Flare), Wet n Wild Comet Couture/Moon Flare; include Shine by SD/Touch of Glam.
    - Greens: Clionadh (Calligraphy, Hedge Maze, Vineyard, Vault, Niello), chartreuse options; include smooth vs textured finishes.
    - Iridescents: Dieverune Dew Drops 1 & 2; Terra Moons Pastel Perihelion shimmers (Nimbus, Between Stars, Rayleigh Scatter).
  - Alt Lips:
    - Grey/black glosses (MOB The Void, Glisten Veil/Gray Area, Kaleidos Stranger); metallic blue (Unearthly Glacial Bite); drugstore frosts suited to deeper tones (WnW Cashmere; Revlon Iced Mocha; CoverGirl Iced Mauve).
    - Staining liners (Kulfi analogs until restock; carry Kulfi when available).
    - Cushion/gel sticks (Trixie Slix + Lip Sync liners; MBM Lip Sync creams as pale-layer bases).
  - Palettes with cool/grey stories:
    - BBB x Freya Yin & Yang (warn about tonal subtlety on deeper skin; pair with deeper singles).
    - Gourmande Girls Apparition; Melt Evergreens mini stack; Kryptein Supernova.
  - Fun tools/accessories:
    - Face/eye stamps; colored mascaras (red/blue); white/blue liners for whimsical looks.

- Content Strategy:
  - Replicate “POV: you’re looking for X chrome” comparison reels with mirror-in-frame and face swatches for real-world payoff.
  - Undertone guidance: “smooth vs textured,” “sheer vs opaque,” and “best bases to pull shift” (e.g., yellow base to pull TM Eneve).
  - Alt lip bar: show 3‑step mixes (stain liner + taupe/grey cream + gloss) as quick shorts; weartest check-ins.
  - Inclusivity spotlights: “Frosts for brown skin” and “Cool greys on deep skin—how to build depth.”
  - Skill snacks: brush-pressure demos, eyeliner-as-eyeshadow tricks, lower-lash color placement.

- Gap Analysis:
  - Distribution: stock hard-to-find Wet n Wild Chameleon Chromes and NYX collab items regionally.
  - Grey/black gloss scarcity: expand shade ladder from taupe‑grey to near‑black.
  - Staining lip liners: broader shade range to mitigate Kulfi stockouts.
  - Cool-tone depth: pair grey palettes with deeper companion mattes for dark skin shoppers.
  - Ethics friction (Sephora Collection thread): highlight cruelty-free alternatives in parallel assortments.

## 6. Special insights

- Emerging Micro-Trends:
  - Grey gloss as the new “nude” in alt looks; black gloss going medium-opacity for 90s softness.
  - Yellow chromes gaining traction (TM Eneve mention and creator excitement).
  - Red lower-lash accents and colored mascaras as editorial signatures.
  - Stamps/tattoo stamps and “doodle liner” returning for whimsical, low-effort impact.

- Unexpected Pairings:
  - Eyeliners repurposed as eyeshadows (Melt Colibri; liquid shadows as smoky bases).
  - Super Shock Shadows used on lips/highlight; highlighters used as lid toppers (Haus Labs Fire Opal; Wet n Wild Solar Ember).
  - Lip contouring with matte shadows (black/grey) to create velvet/ombre lips.

- Creative insight:
  - Build “Seasonal Chrome Capsules” (Warm—Oranges/Peaches; Cool—Greens/Mints; Pastel—Iridescents) bundled with recommended matte bases and glitter fixatives to control texture and fallout.
  - Launch an “Alt Nude Lip Lab” merchandised by undertone (taupe‑grey ladder), with preset trios: stain liner + pale matte cream + veil gloss; include a wear-test pledge clip.
  - Whimsy Kits: white/sky-blue liners, stamp pen, micro-pearls, and a sheer sparkle topper—paired with a 60‑second “fish in a pond” challenge to spark UGC.
