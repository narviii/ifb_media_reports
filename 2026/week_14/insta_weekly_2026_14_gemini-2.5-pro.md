# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

*   **Key Takeaway:** The week ending April 5, 2026, saw a significant push towards cool-toned grunge and alternative aesthetics, with gray lip gloss from Glisten Cosmetics emerging as a viral product. Creators heavily featured blue/purple chrome eyeshadows and moody, muted color stories, signaling a clear rejection of typical spring pastels.

## 1. Overview

*   **The Vibe:** The feed was dominated by video content, particularly tutorial-style Reels and first-impression reviews. The focus was on deconstructing specific, often unconventional, product categories like gray lip gloss, chromatic highlighters, and cool-toned contour palettes. There was a strong educational undercurrent, with creators demonstrating nuanced application techniques (blush placement, lip contouring) rather than just showcasing finished looks.

*   **Engagement Temperature:** Engagement was exceptionally high around new and niche product launches. Comments were less about general compliments and more focused on direct purchase intent ("I need this," "Think you’ve just convinced me to buy this gloss"). There was a palpable sense of community-driven discovery, with followers asking for specific tutorials, comparing formulas, and recommending alternatives in comment threads.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

*   **Top Mentioned Brands:**
    *   **Glisten Cosmetics:** Highest velocity this week, driven by the viral gray lip gloss launch.
    *   **Danessa Myricks Beauty:** Strong, consistent mentions for base products and multi-use pigments.
    *   **Blend Bunny Cosmetics:** Gaining traction with the new Jordan Liberty collaboration palettes and pencils.
    *   **Melt Cosmetics:** Featured for its grungy, autumnal color stories ("Twelfth Hour," "Evergreens").
    *   **Terra Moons Cosmetics:** A go-to for high-impact, sparkly single eyeshadows.
    *   **Dieverune Cosmetics:** Noted for its cool-toned, moody palettes.

*   **Viral Products:**
    *   **Glisten Cosmetics Glis Gloss in "Veil" & "Gray Area":** The definitive viral product of the week. Multiple dedicated videos from creator Courtney Quarantine sparked intense excitement and explicit purchase intent. The novelty of a high-quality, non-streaky gray gloss drove the conversation.
    *   **Blue/Purple Chrome Eyeshadows (Category):** This entire category saw a surge. Creator `glamsmitty`'s multi-post comparison of shades from Terra Moons, Clionadh, Half Magic, and Karla Cosmetics acted as a catalyst, generating significant discussion around formula, texture, and sparkle factor.
    *   **Danessa Myricks Beauty Yummy Skin Mini Kits:** A high-engagement video highlighted the value and performance of these travel-sized kits, positioning them as an accessible entry point to the popular line.

*   **The "Look" Breakdown:**
    *   **EYES:** A clear shift toward high-impact, dimensional shimmers. The focus was on multichromes and duochromes, particularly in blue, purple, and green shifts. Matte looks were used to support these shimmers, often featuring muted, murky greens (Melt) or cool grays (Dieverune).
    *   **LIPS:** Unconventional colors reign. Gray gloss is the hero trend, followed by vampy browns, deep plums, and muted mauves. The application is nuanced, often involving a "lip contour" with a taupe pencil before layering multiple products to create a dimensional, ombré effect.
    *   **CHEEKS:** Technique over color. The conversation centered on strategic placement for sculpting, as demonstrated by Robert Welsh. The preferred shades are cool-toned mauves and blush-bronzer hybrids that add structure without overt color.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

*   **Visual Themes:** **#gothmakeup**, **#grungemakeup**, and **#softgoth** dominated the landscape. This "Gloomy Spring" aesthetic favors moody, complex looks over simple, bright ones. The vibe is sophisticated, slightly melancholic, and highly artistic.

*   **Color Stories:**
    *   **Grayscale & Taupe:** The core of the week's trend. Gray is the new neutral, appearing in lip gloss, contour, and eyeshadow, often paired with desaturated taupes and mauves.
    *   **Murky Greens & Oranges:** Driven by Melt Cosmetics' collections, this palette combines deep olive and forest greens with burnt oranges for a distinctly autumnal, grungy feel that defies the season.
    *   **Chromatic Blues & Purples:** The standout accent color story. Creators are layering iridescent pigments to create ethereal, high-shine eye looks that shift from periwinkle to deep indigo.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

*   **Stocking Recommendations:**
    *   **Prioritize Gray Lip Gloss:** This is the week's highest-velocity trend. Secure stock of **Glisten Cosmetics' "Veil" and "Gray Area"** immediately.
    *   **Expand Blue/Purple Chromes:** Curate a diverse selection of blue-to-purple shifting eyeshadows. Offer various formulas (pressed singles, loose pigments, liquid creams) from key brands like **Terra Moons, Clionadh, and Karla Cosmetics**.
    *   **Curate a "Gloomy Spring" Collection:** Build a product assortment around muted mauves, murky olive greens, and cool-toned taupes. Feature palettes like **Dieverune's "Silver and Cold"** and **Melt's "Evergreens."**
    *   **Introduce Shimmery Lip Liners:** The **Moira Cosmetics** shimmery liner represents a new texture trend. Stocking this or similar products offers a unique item that aligns with the current focus on dimensional lips.

*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   **Lead with Video Tutorials:** Reels demonstrating product application and first impressions are driving sales. Static flatlays are supplementary.
    *   **Create Comparison Content:** Produce videos comparing different formulas within a trending category (e.g., "The Best Blue/Purple Chromes," "Cool-Toned Contours Compared"). This builds authority and directly answers audience questions.
    *   **Focus on Problem-Solving:** Create content that addresses specific community pain points, such as "How to find a mauve blush that doesn't turn orange" or "The best way to clean up eyeshadow fallout."

*   **Gap Analysis:**
    *   **The Mauve Blush Problem:** There is a clear, stated demand for a truly cool-toned, gray-based mauve blush that does not pull warm on fair or olive skin tones. Sourcing and heavily marketing such a product would fill a significant market gap.
    *   **Allergen-Conscious Formulations:** A creator's rant about sweet almond oil in cosmetics highlights a growing concern. Highlighting products that are free from common allergens could be a powerful and unique selling proposition.

## 6. Special insights

*   **Emerging Micro-Trends:**
    *   **The Gray Lip Contour:** Before applying color, creators are using a matte, fair taupe lip pencil to neutralize their natural lip color and create a perfect canvas for gray or cool-toned lipsticks. This is a pro-level technique moving into the mainstream.
    *   **Shimmery Lip Liners:** The appearance of a shimmery lip liner from Moira Cosmetics signals a potential shift in texture play, moving beyond the standard matte liner/glossy center combination to a fully reflective lip look.

*   **Unexpected Pairings:**
    *   **Gel Eyeliner as Lip Art:** Creator `s.andromedae` used Melt Cosmetics' green gel eyeliners blended into the center of a nude lipstick to create a custom green ombré lip. This demonstrates a high level of creativity and the multi-use potential of pigmented gel formulas.
    *   **Green Pencil as a Lip Base:** The use of a green Kryolan pencil under a sheer lip product to create a custom, muted olive green lip color shows that consumers are willing to "cocktail" products from different categories to achieve their desired unique shade.

*   **Creative insight:** The "Gloomy Spring" aesthetic is a consumer-led rebellion against traditional seasonal marketing. It reflects a desire for more personal, sophisticated, and complex color stories that indie brands are uniquely positioned to fulfill. The intense interest in "goth-friendly" Japanese drugstore finds (Kate Lip Monster) proves there is a global appetite for accessible, high-quality alternative makeup that major Western drugstore brands are failing to meet.
