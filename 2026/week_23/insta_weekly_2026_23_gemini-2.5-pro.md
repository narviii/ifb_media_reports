# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

*   **Key Takeaway:** For the week of June 1-7, 2026, the indie beauty landscape was defined by a stark duality: a surge in earthy, muted `#dirtcore` and grunge aesthetics ran parallel to an explosion of vibrant, sparkly "summer fairy" looks, driven by major new collection launches from Blend Bunny, Terra Moons, and Bella Beaute Bar.

## 1. Overview

*   **The Vibe:** The feed was a tale of two extremes. One side embraced a moody, atmospheric grunge with olive greens, rusty browns, and unconventional grey and blue lips. The other side celebrated the start of Pride month and summer with hyper-saturated rainbow mattes and high-impact, multi-dimensional shimmers. Video content dominated, with creators favoring in-depth tutorials for complex graphic liner looks and swatch-heavy reels to announce and review a wave of new indie collections.

*   **Engagement Temperature:** Highly active and positive. Followers were deeply engaged with new launches, asking specific questions about formulas, performance ("Does it crease?"), and potential dupes. Compliments were specific, moving beyond "pretty" to "That blend is fire" or "The blue reflect on the lips😍," indicating a knowledgeable and discerning audience.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

*   **Top Mentioned Brands:**
    *   **Blend Bunny® Cosmetics:** Huge momentum from the new **Psilocybin Collection**. Featured in unboxings, tutorials, and swatch videos across multiple creators.
    *   **Terra Moons Cosmetics:** The **Space Candy** collection drove significant conversation, with creators showcasing its intensely sparkly, shifty single shadows.
    *   **Bella Beaute Bar:** The upcoming **Arch of Color** collection received a coordinated PR push, with its massive all-matte rainbow palette and accompanying shimmer singles generating major buzz.
    *   **Rituel de Fille:** Consistently mentioned as a go-to for base products (primer, contour) and unique cream blushes.
    *   **Marc Jacobs Beauty:** The brand's relaunch is a key talking point, with creators and audiences excited by the playful new packaging and high-sparkle eyeshadow singles.

*   **Viral Products:**
    *   **Blend Bunny Psilocybin Palette:** The week's hero product. Its unique color story of neons, greens, and periwinkles inspired multiple complex, fairy-like graphic liner looks.
    *   **Terra Moons "Space Candy" Singles:** Specific shades like `Wildberry Wormhole` and `Limelight` were called out for their unique shifts and textures. The entire collection feeds the demand for high-impact, special effect toppers.
    *   **ColourPop Jelly Much Eyeshadow Sticks:** Generated significant discussion around their performance-to-price value, with creators comparing them directly to higher-end alternatives from Danessa Myricks and Half Magic.
    *   **Urban Decay "Gash" Eyeshadow:** The return of this iconic 90s shade tapped into powerful nostalgia, driving conversation even if the excitement was more about novelty than formula innovation.

*   **The "Look" Breakdown:**
    *   **EYES:** A clear split between muted grunge (smoky olives, deep browns) and vibrant psychedelia (neon greens, pink-to-blue gradients). High-impact, wet-look shimmers and duochromes were layered over both styles.
    *   **LIPS:** Unconventional colors are trending heavily. Deep, cool-toned lips—including greys, muted blues, and olive greens—were a staple of the grunge aesthetic. High-shine glosses and balms provided a textural counterpoint, with Bella Beaute Bar's new sparkly balms in teal and orange standing out.
    *   **CHEEKS:** Cream and liquid formulas reign. A focus on cool-toned pinks and dusty mauves was evident, with creators swatching and comparing multiple options for a flushed, dewy look.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

*   **Visual Themes:**
    *   **#dirtcore & #grungemakeup:** The most cohesive aesthetic trend of the week. Characterized by earthy, "rusty" tones, smudgy application, and unconventional lip colors. It feels like a more refined, textural evolution of 90s grunge.
    *   **#fairymakeup & Psychedelia:** Driven by the Blend Bunny launch, this aesthetic features vibrant, almost neon pastel gradients, heavy glitter, and whimsical graphic liner shapes resembling butterfly wings.
    *   **Ethereal Blues:** A recurring theme of celestial, cool-toned blue and purple looks, often inspired by fantasy concepts like video games (`Dark Souls`) and featuring iridescent, high-shine finishes.

*   **Color Stories:**
    *   **Earthy & Muted:** Olive green, terracotta, deep rust, charcoal, and taupe.
    *   **Vibrant Rainbow:** Saturated neons and primary colors, particularly in matte formulas, are being used for complex, multi-color blends.
    *   **Cool-Toned Shimmers:** Icy blues, periwinkles, and lavenders with strong duochrome shifts are the preferred toppers and inner-corner highlights.

## 4. Purchase-Intent Signals (From Comments)

*   **"Add to Cart" Energy:** High intent was shown for entire collections. Comments like "I might need this" and "Are these limited edition?" on Blend Bunny and Bella Beaute Bar posts were common. On a lip swatch post for NABLA, a user commented, "The last two are to die for & were already in my chart."

*   **Restock Demands:** A clear signal was sent for **Rituel de Fille's "Snowdrop" blush**, with a user commenting, "Ah, I wish @ritueldefille would bring back snowdrop!"

*   **Curiosity Clusters:**
    *   **Formula Performance:** The most common questions centered on wear time and texture. "Did they wear well during the day or did they cause creasing?" (re: ColourPop sticks) and "How did you find the wear time on these?" (re: Marc Jacobs shadows).
    *   **Product Identification:** "i neeed the pallete what is it?" and "Hi, ❤️where did you buy the white base foundation" show viewers are looking to replicate entire looks, not just admire them.
    *   **Dupe & Comparison Questions:** Sophisticated buyers asked for direct comparisons, such as whether a new Sheen Effect shadow was similar to a specific shade in a Danessa Myricks palette.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

*   **Stocking Recommendations:**
    *   **Prioritize Unconventional Lip Colors:** There is a clear, demonstrated demand for grungy, muted lipsticks in shades of grey, olive, deep brown, and cool-toned blues. Stocking these will cater directly to the #dirtcore trend.
    *   **Invest in High-Impact Single Shadows:** The excitement around Terra Moons proves the market for special, sparkly topper shades is strong. Focus on duochromes and multichromes with unique color shifts.
    *   **Curate by Color Story:** Create dedicated sections for "Ethereal Blues & Purples" and "Earthy Grunge" to help customers shop these trending aesthetics.
    *   **Expand Cream Blush Offerings:** Focus on cool-toned pinks and mauves in cream/liquid formulas.

*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   **Create Comparison Reels:** Address audience questions head-on by creating short videos comparing popular products (e.g., "Drugstore vs. Indie Eyeshadow Sticks"). This builds trust and provides direct value.
    *   **Develop "Get The Look" Tutorials:** Film tutorials for the top trending aesthetics (e.g., "How to Achieve the #dirtcore Lip," "3-Step Ethereal Blue Eye"). Tag the products used and link directly to them.
    *   **Showcase Swatches on Multiple Skin Tones:** The swatch-heavy content from creators like `glamsmitty` and `deandranicoleee` is invaluable. Replicating this with store products will drive conversion.

*   **Gap Analysis:** Creators expressed frustration with brands sending PR without researching their content style. This signals that authenticity is paramount. The store can lean into this by partnering with creators for honest, critical reviews, which audiences clearly value over generic praise.

## 6. Special insights

*   **Emerging Micro-Trends:**
    *   **Monochromatic Mascara:** Creators are matching their mascara color to their graphic liner or eyeshadow (e.g., blue mascara with blue liner, yellow mascara on lower lashes). This is a subtle but impactful technique that adds a cohesive, editorial touch.
    *   **#dirtcore Aesthetic:** This specific hashtag is gaining traction among key alternative influencers. It's more than just grunge; it focuses on textures that mimic rust, soil, and metal, pairing muted mattes with unexpected shimmers.

*   **Unexpected Pairings:**
    *   **Multi-use Pigments:** Creators are using products outside their intended categories to achieve specific effects. `s.andromedae` used a face highlighter on the lips for a duochrome finish, while `freya86_ff` used eyeshadow from a palette as a lip color. This highlights the versatility customers seek.

*   **Creative insight:**
    *   **The Power of Nostalgia:** The successful relaunch of Marc Jacobs Beauty and the buzz around Urban Decay's "Gash" eyeshadow are fueled by millennial nostalgia. Similarly, the Bella Beaute Bar "Arch of Color" palette was explicitly linked to the "scene girl era." This emotional connection is a powerful marketing tool that can be leveraged through "throwback" themed content and product curation.
    *   **C-Beauty's Growing Influence:** Brands like Sugarock and Cheeryep are appearing frequently, tagged with `#cbeauty`. Their unique, often ethereal or grungy aesthetics and highly ornate packaging are directly influencing the visual language of Western indie beauty, pushing the boundaries of both product design and makeup artistry.
