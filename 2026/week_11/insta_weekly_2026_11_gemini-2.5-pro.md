# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

*   **Key Takeaway:** For the week ending March 15, 2026, the indie beauty landscape was dominated by major creator collaborations, particularly the **Unearthly Cosmetics x s.andromedae "Woeful Whimsy" palette**, which drove significant pre-launch and launch day buzz. A clear aesthetic split emerged between whimsical, colorful graphic liner looks and a resurgence of moody, cool-toned grunge and goth aesthetics, with a strong emphasis on high-impact, multi-dimensional eyeshadow finishes.

## 1. Overview

*   **The Vibe:** The week was intensely focused on new product launches and creator-led curation. The feed was a mix of highly anticipated palette reveals, detailed swatch videos (both on-arm and on-eye), and creative tutorials showcasing the new products in action. The energy was less about everyday looks and more about editorial expression and product discovery.

*   **Engagement Temperature:** Audience engagement was exceptionally high and specific. Followers were not just passively consuming content; they were actively asking questions about launch times, formula textures, and potential dupes. Comments sections were filled with excitement, purchase declarations for new palettes, and strong support for creator collaborations.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

*   **Top Mentioned Brands:**
    *   **Indie Leaders:** Unearthly Cosmetics, Glisten Cosmetics, Touch of Glam Beauty, Dieverune Cosmetics, Fantasy Cosmetica, Terra Moons Cosmetics, and Simply Posh Cosmetics were at the forefront due to new launches and frequent use.
    *   **Mainstream Staples:** Danessa Myricks Beauty, Huda Beauty, MAC Cosmetics, and Kryolan were consistently used for base products, liners, and core staples.

*   **Viral Products:**
    *   **Unearthly Cosmetics x s.andromedae "Woeful Whimsy" Palette:** The undisputed star of the week. Multiple creators posted swatches and tutorials, generating massive hype. The unique color story of pastels and grungy neutrals resonated strongly.
    *   **Fantasy Cosmetica "Cleric" Palette:** Gained significant traction for its moody, cool-toned green and blue color story, appealing to the grunge aesthetic.
    *   **Touch of Glam Beauty "Midnight in Aspen" Palette:** Drove conversation with its collection of intense, sparkly holographic and multi-chrome shimmers.
    *   **Glisten Cosmetics Juicy Tint in "Bayberry" (Gray):** A single video from Courtney Quarantine created a viral moment around this unconventional gray lip tint, sparking immediate purchase intent for its unique "corpse glam" effect.

*   **The "Look" Breakdown:**
    *   **EYES:** The focus was on texture and finish. High-impact, sparkly, and shifting eyeshadows (duochromes, holos) were dominant. Grungy greens, cool-toned blues, and silvers were prevalent. Graphic liner, often incorporating stamps and floral motifs, was a key technique.
    *   **LIPS:** A strong trend toward vampy, glossy lips in shades of deep brown, plum, and oxblood. Cool-toned gray and taupe lipsticks also appeared frequently. The use of lip tints for a stained base under gloss is a growing technique.
    *   **CHEEKS:** While eyes and lips were dramatic, blush was often a soft, diffused matte pink. The exception was the use of unconventional eyeshadow shades, like neon green, as blush for a more editorial effect.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

*   **Visual Themes:** The feed was a battleground of aesthetics.
    *   **#gothmakeup & #grungemakeup:** Characterized by cool-toned smoky eyes, vampy lips, and a moody, ethereal vibe. Palettes like Fantasy Cosmetica's "Cleric" and lip products from Necromancy Cosmetica fueled this.
    *   **#graphicliner & #flowereyeliner:** A more whimsical, colorful trend led by creators like Lindsey Rowley, using products from Glisten Cosmetics to create intricate, artistic eye looks.
    *   **#etherealmakeup & #sparklymakeup:** A finish-focused trend centered on iridescent, multi-chrome, and holographic toppers from brands like Touch of Glam Beauty and Terra Moons Cosmetics.

*   **Color Stories:**
    *   **Grungy Greens & Browns:** A major theme, seen in swatch carousels and tutorials. Shades ranged from olive and khaki to deep forest green with brown duochrome shifts.
    *   **Cool-Toned Blues & Silvers:** Driven by new palette releases, this color story brought a moody, celestial feel to many looks.
    *   **Whimsical Pastels:** The "Woeful Whimsy" palette introduced a color story of bright pinks, purples, and lime greens grounded by soft grays and neutrals.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

*   **Stocking Recommendations:**
    *   **Must-Have:** Prioritize multi-dimensional eyeshadows: duochromes, holos, and intense shimmers are driving sales. Palettes with curated, unique color stories (grungy greens, cool-toned blues) are outperforming standard neutral palettes.
    *   **Trending:** Stock lip tints in unconventional shades (gray, taupe) and graphic liner tools like stamps and water-activated liners.
    *   **Key Items:** Unearthly Cosmetics "Woeful Whimsy", Fantasy Cosmetica "Cleric", Touch of Glam Beauty "Midnight in Aspen", Glisten Cosmetics Juicy Tints & Spectra Stamps.

*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   **High-Performing Format:** Tutorial-style Reels demonstrating how to use complex eyeshadows and create graphic liner looks generate the highest views and engagement.
    *   **Launch Strategy:** Carousel posts featuring high-quality arm and eye swatches are essential for driving hype around new product launches. The "POV: you're looking for a..." swatch comparison format is highly effective for showcasing product categories.

*   **Gap Analysis:** Creator s.andromedae's post (DV2lWhWjRJR) about the industry's relentless push for video content—and her decision to step back due to health accessibility issues—highlights a significant pain point for photo-focused creators. This presents an opportunity to champion and amplify still-image artistry, which clearly has a dedicated audience.

## 6. Special insights

*   **Emerging Micro-Trends:**
    *   **Unconventional Blush Placement/Color:** The use of eyeshadow as blush, specifically neon green (`s.andromedae` using "Gumdrop"), is a nascent editorial trend trickling into the indie space.
    *   **The "Bruised" Lip Tint:** The excitement around Glisten's gray lip tint points to a micro-trend of "corpse glam" or "bruised" lip effects, moving beyond typical berry stains to more complex, cool-toned stains.

*   **Unexpected Pairings:**
    *   **Highlighter as Lip Topper:** `s.andromedae` used a Dieverune Cosmetics highlighter over lipstick to create a custom duochrome lip, showcasing product versatility.

*   **Creative insight:**
    *   **The Creator as A&R:** This week proves that the most successful indie launches are deeply collaborative. Creators like s.andromedae are not just marketing vehicles; they are acting as product developers and curators, creating color stories the community is craving. The success of these collabs is a testament to trusting creator vision.
    *   **Technique Over Tools:** While new products drove the conversation, educational content from creators like Robert Welsh on *how* to apply makeup (e.g., using less powder and concealer) received massive engagement. This signals a mature audience that is interested not just in acquiring new products, but in mastering the techniques to use them effectively.
