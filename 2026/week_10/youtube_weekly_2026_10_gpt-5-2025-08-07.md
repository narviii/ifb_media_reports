# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway: For the week of March 2–8, 2026, indie color continues to dominate with a marked cool‑toned revival (silver/teal/purple) alongside a persistent green wave. Texture talk (multichromes and chunky shimmers) and alt lip combos drove purchase intent, while new indie launches (Dieverune 3/6, Unearthly x Andromeda 3/12) concentrated attention.

## 1. Overview

- The week felt distinctly cool‑toned and alt: silvers, teals, icy highlighters, and vampy purples took center stage. Green remained the anchor hue across multiple creators (from swampy/grungy to neon “slime”).
- Texture management was a recurring conversation. High‑impact, flaky multichromes were celebrated for payoff but flagged as “high‑maintenance,” pushing glitter/shimmer primers and sticky bases into the spotlight.
- Lip content leaned into “combo culture”: deep liners plus greige/purple center shades and high‑shine toppers. These shorts drive multiproduct carts for indie lip liners, liquid lips, and glosses.
- Customization returned via singles (Nabla Liberty X; Oden’s Eye x Heather singles), but there’s friction: several creators still prefer curated palettes, indicating singles sell when bundled smartly or with clear use cases.
- Mindful consumption and “shop your stash” formats (palette roulette, throwbacks) fostered brand rediscovery (Nomad, Unearthly) while still feeding targeted hype for new launches (Dieverune; Unearthly x Andromeda).

  - Dieverune: Silver & Cold palette (launch 3/6), Bizarre Botanicals palette, Grimfrost highlighter, face gems. Sentiment: strongly positive (cool‑tone cohesion, shimmer reflect, goth aesthetic).
  - Unearthly: Sorcerous Smoke (moody greens), Dead Cute (neon green), upcoming Woeful Whimsy x Andromeda (3/12). Sentiment: positive; neons blendable; shimmers impactful.
  - Nomad: Multiple palette spotlights (Berlin Underground, Polar Express, Hudson Valley, Pacific Northwest, Fêtes de Provence; Ireland Atlantic Way). Sentiment: positive; re‑editions praised; mustard/reds and grungy tones resonant.
  - Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin: Spring Butterfly singles bundle and looks. Sentiment: positive; high pigment and easy 3–4 shade looks; singles positioned as versatile.
  - Bella Beaute Bar: Dead Roses Vol. 2 (top pick), Dead Roses highlighter, Sparkle Balms, Shimmer Stay primer. Sentiment: consistently positive; “must‑have” language.
  - Black Moon + Kaleidos lip ecosystem: Black Moon lip pencils (Tortie, Underworld) and Bones liquid lip; Kaleidos Lip Clay (Siren) + liners. Sentiment: positive; high‑performing, alt‑leaning shades.
  - Adept Sapphire Selene: Mixed; color story and packaging applauded, but chunky shimmers deemed fussy without glitter primer.
  - Dede/DeDe’s Signature: Sand Seal/Honey Hold eye bases. Sentiment: positive; reliable grip and tone match.
  - Judydoll Curling Iron Mascara: Standout for lash hold. Sentiment: very positive repeat endorsement.

- Sentiment patterns and context:

  - Mixed/negative notes: Adept shimmer textures require technique/time (glitter primer recommended). Nabla Art of Skin Contour Stick criticized as stiff/patchy; ColourPop lipstick applicator called awkward; Lethal gloss brush tip disliked; Melt Ultra Pinks darkest shade finicky.

- Hype cycle and positioning:
  - Early hype/launch: Dieverune Silver & Cold (3/6) with strong swatch‑driven interest; Unearthly x Andromeda (3/12) teaser‑led momentum; Adept Sapphire Selene on preorder with caveats.
  - Default comparisons and anchors: Bella Beaute Bar Sparkle Balms/Dead Roses as sparkle/highlight benchmarks; Nomad as the “reliable indie palette” standard; glitter primer as the expected partner for textured shimmers.

- Product combos creators pushed:

  - Cheeks: Likely Makeup Poppy Cream Blush + Clown Blush Palette (cream‑to‑powder “blush sandwich”).
  - Eyes: Sticky bases (Dede, Sigma, Melt) + shimmer enhancers (Bella Beaute Bar Shimmer Stay, NYX Glitter Primer, Clionadh Shine On) paired with high‑shine indie shimmers/multichromes.

## 3. Category Trends

- Eyeshadows: Dominant. Two strong currents—cool‑toned revival (silvers, teals, icy whites, purples) and the entrenched green trend (swampy/grungy through neon slime). Graphic liners created from deep mattes noted.
- Lips: Alt/goth combos with deep liners, greige/purple centers, and glassy toppers; matte‑to‑gloss transformations are sticky content drivers.
- Blush: High‑pigment cream layered with powder for editorial “cherry” looks; nose/temple placement persists.
- Highlighter: Icy/duochrome shifts (blue‑teal) with smooth, high‑shine payoff gaining attention.
- Bases/primers: Eyeshadow bases and glitter/shimmer primers are essential enablers for textured formulas; repeated across creators.
- Fatigue/skepticism: Singles bundles interest tempered by some creators’ preference for complete palettes. All‑matte/all‑shimmer formats get kudos but mixed‑finish palettes remain more usable day‑to‑day. Chunky shimmer textures perceived as effortful.

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- Cool‑toned revival across creators:
  - Dieverune Silver & Cold (teal/silver/icy whites), ColourPop Twilight Eclipse (cool reds/greys/silvers), Amy’s Adept look (green/purple cool story), multiple icy highlighter moments.
- Persistent green fixation:
  - Battybean’s top 10 green palettes and green tutorials; Unearthly’s neon lime looks; Nomad’s greens featured repeatedly. Preference skews “swampy/grungy” over clean brights, but neons also surged.
- Texture management as a prerequisite:
  - Consistent use of glitter/shimmer primers (NYX Glitter Primer, Bella Beaute Bar Shimmer Stay, Clionadh Shine On) and sticky bases (Sigma, Dede, Melt) to control chunky multichromes; positioned as must‑haves.
- Customization vs convenience:
  - Singles resurgence (Nabla Liberty X; Oden’s Eye Spring Butterfly singles) with educational framing; some pushback preferring ready‑made palettes. Opportunity for curated quad builders and suggested shade recipes.
- Nostalgia/alt aesthetics as sales levers:
  - Pop culture tie‑ins (Twilight), spooky‑cute/circus themes, goblincore/botanical, goth glam packaging—consumers respond to themed storytelling plus high reflect textures.
- Regional signals:
  - European indie momentum: Nabla (Italy) reconfirmed “still good” with caveats; Oden’s Eye (Sweden) collab visibility high; Nomad (US indie) treated as quality benchmark.
  - C‑beauty adoption: Judydoll mascara repeatedly recommended for curl retention; Kaleidos lip clays and liners consistently used in alt combos. Western creators actively normalizing C‑beauty staples.

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- Strong triggers:
  - Launch/urgency: Dieverune (3/6) and Unearthly x Andromeda (3/12) dates repeatedly highlighted; Adept on preorder.
  - Superlatives/endorsements: “Ultimate grungy,” “favorite of the month,” “holy grail” (One Size Powder), “never creases” (Nabla Cupid’s Arrow), “keeps lashes curled all day” (Judydoll).
  - Sensory/visual hooks: High‑shine, multichrome shifts on camera; icy/ethereal highlights; luxury packaging (Adept).
  - Problem/solution framing: Glitter primer eliminates fallout; true brown liner; customizable singles/quads.
  - Bundle thinking: Lip combos and blush layering invite multiproduct carts.

- Dampeners:
  - Adept’s chunky textures flagged as effortful for “lazy makeup” days—necessitates expectation setting and accessory primers.
  - Nabla contour stick called stiff/patchy; some applicator complaints (ColourPop lip, Lethal gloss brush).

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- What to stock now (high momentum):
  - Cool‑toned eye edits: Curate “Silver & Cold” assortments—teal, silver, icy white multichromes; include smooth high‑reflect toppers and deep teal/navy mattes.
  - Green capsule: “Swamp Witch” and “Neon Slime” edits—grungy greens (forest/olive/murky) plus a neon lime option; include contrasting warm anchors.
  - Texture enablers: NYX Glitter Primer or indie equivalents, Bella Beaute Bar Shimmer Stay, sticky eye bases (Sigma, Dede/DeDe’s, Melt); merchandize as mandatory add‑ons with textured shadows.
  - Alt lip ecosystem: Deep brown/purple/blackened liners (Black Moon‑style), cool‑greige/purple soft‑matte lips (Kaleidos Lip Clay), clear/high‑shine toppers; sell as pre‑built “Vampy Ombré Kits.”
  - Highlighters: Icy/blue‑teal shifting powders with smooth, non‑gritty finish; position for cool‑tone looks.
  - Mascara that holds curl: If possible, carry Judydoll Curling Iron Mascara or a functionally similar SKU; message the curl‑hold benefit prominently.

- Launch and halo plays:
  - If distribution allows: prioritize Dieverune (Silver & Cold; highlighters) and Unearthly (Dead Cute; Woeful Whimsy 3/12) with timed landing pages and waitlist capture. If not, build timely “dupe‑by‑vibe” bundles with indie alternatives and SEO around launch names.
  - Support Oden’s Eye singles behavior: offer quad builders, discounted bundles (e.g., “4‑pan starter” with two mattes + two shimmers) and pre‑mapped recipes for 3–4 shade looks.

- Bundling and education:
  - Texture Control Sets: palette + glitter primer + flat shader brush, with guidance on application (tap, finger use, base choice).
  - Blush Layering Duos: high‑pigment cream + complementary powder in berry/cherry tones; include how‑to for longevity.
  - “Alt Lip Trio”: liner + center matte + gloss topper; showcase creator‑inspired shade maps (grunge nude; vamp purple).

- Gaps to fill / avoid:
  - Avoid or soft‑position products with repeating negatives: stiff/patchy contour sticks; brush‑tip glosses; awkward lip applicators. If stocked, add clear usage tips or comparison guides.
  - Singles‑only sets can be slower; improve velocity via curated quads, magnets, and creator‑inspired shade combos.
  - For chunky textured palettes (e.g., Adept‑like): set expectations on product pages, auto‑recommend glitter primer, and add a “quick‑use” alt (smoother topper) for low‑effort users.

- Regional expansion:
  - Lean into EU and C‑beauty that creators already normalized: consider Nabla complexion/lips and Oden’s Eye (if available), Kaleidos lip clays/liners, Judydoll mascara. Highlight origin stories where it adds trust/novelty.

- Merchandising themes:
  - Create navigational tiles: Goth Glam, Spooky‑Cute Circus, Botanical/Goblincore, Silver & Cold, Swamp Witch, Neon Slime.
  - Seasonal shift prep: promote “Spring Butterfly” aesthetics—earthy greens with airy shimmers and river‑fairy neutrals—to bridge cool‑tone winter to spring/summer neons.

Direct, product‑first curation plus technique‑forward education will convert this week’s heavy swatch/texture intrigue into carts, particularly via bundles that mirror creator combos and include the necessary primers.
