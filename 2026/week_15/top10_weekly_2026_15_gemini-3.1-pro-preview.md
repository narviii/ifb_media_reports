# Weekly Top 10 Indie Makeup Product Mentions

**Reporting Period:** April 06–12, 2026

1. **Eye Primer by Lethal Cosmetics** — 6 mentions  
   Creators used both the transparent and white versions of this primer as a reliable eye base to ensure eyeshadow performance and longevity. It was specifically praised for its ability to make pastel shades pop to their fullest potential without altering their true colors.

2. **Spectra Pencil by Glisten Cosmetics** — 6 mentions  
   These pencils were frequently utilized by creators for precise, colorful graphic liner work and detailed eye looks. Available in shades like white, black, and dark blue, they were highlighted for their versatility, often being layered with glosses or used to add cool-toned dimension to lip combinations.

3. **Speckled Pens by Glisten Cosmetics** — 6 mentions  
   Creators consistently reached for these pens—particularly in the shades taupe and russet—to add faux freckles to their makeup routines. The pens were favored for creating a natural, sun-kissed, and subtly grungy aesthetic that perfectly complemented dewy skin finishes.

4. **Apparition Palette by Gourmande Girls** — 5 mentions  
   Described as the perfect "pastel sister" to the brand's Haunted palette, this release was highly praised for its buttery mattes and high-shine shimmers. Creators loved its "spooky spring" theme, noting that it beautifully balances traditional pastels with deeper, murky shades for versatile looks.

5. **Levitation Lash Mascara by Rituel de Fille** — 5 mentions  
   This mascara was a staple in several creators' routines, often used to complete bold, editorial, and grungy eye looks. It was relied upon to provide dark, voluminous, and wispy lashes that stood out against highly reflective or colorful multichrome eyeshadows.

6. **Glowing Creature of Light by Rituel de Fille** — 5 mentions  
   Frequently used as a luminous primer, this product was favored for creating a dewy, ethereal base. Creators consistently paired it with soft-matte foundations and cream contours to achieve a highly reflective, glowing complexion that anchored their editorial makeup looks.

7. **The Little Mermaid Multichrome Highlighter by ColourPop** — 5 mentions  
   Despite some notes on its flaky formula, creators were obsessed with the unique, bright, and fun glow this highlighter provided. Shades like "Soft Shell" and "Not a Guppy" were frequently used to add a striking, icy-toned duochrome shift to the cheekbones and inner corners.

8. **Faded Pro Pencil by Blend Bunny** — 4 mentions  
   Part of the Jordan Liberty Masterclass Vol 2 collection, this cool, medium-brown pencil was highly praised for its smudge-proof longevity. Creators loved using it for smoky, grungy everyday eyeliner and waterline definition, noting that once it sets, it does not budge even on oily eyelids.

9. **Lilium Lipstick by Rituel de Fille** — 4 mentions  
   Part of a new lab launch, this "light as a feather" formula was celebrated by creators as a potential new favorite. Described as a neutral shadow peach, users enjoyed wearing it alone or pairing it with a lip liner and dark gloss to add extra depth to soft-goth looks.

10. **Colorfix by Danessa Myricks Beauty** — 4 mentions  
    These liquid pigments were a go-to for creators needing high-impact, versatile color that stays in place. Whether used as a reliable base for half-cut creases or as a standalone shadow, the formula was praised for its blendability and intense, fallout-free payoff.
