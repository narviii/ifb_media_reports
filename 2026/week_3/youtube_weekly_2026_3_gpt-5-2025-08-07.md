# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway (Jan 12–18, 2026): Indie color is in control. Cross‑creator enthusiasm centered on Lethal x SpongeBob and Nomad Paris La Bohème while lip textures split between glossy dark‑liner combos and new blurred liquid mattes; blush layering/draping continued to surge.

## 1. Overview

Creators leaned heavily into maximalist, alt-glam looks: deep smoky mattes topped with high-impact multichromes, glitter gels, and ultra‑reflective toppers. The week felt energetic and launch-driven, with urgency around limited collabs and pre‑launch teasers.

Complexion talk revolved around “glow that behaves”: Armani’s reformulated Luminous Silk and Huda’s pressed powder reformulation drew relief and approval, reframing earlier skepticism about changes to cult formulas.

Lips split into two camps: the indie-favored dark liner + juicy gloss/oil stack, and a mainstream push for soft‑blurred liquid mattes (ColourPop Cloud Whip) that promise comfort over dryness—signaling a matte comeback without the 2016 downsides.

Cheeks were built, not applied: repeated cream‑then‑powder layering and high placement (draping) to lock color and lift the face. Pink/berry monochrome looks tied to Valentine’s timing sat alongside grungy greens/teals.

Meta-narratives—affiliate code transparency, burnout, and anti-consumerist notes—surfaced again, but did not dampen purchase intent when performance and scarcity were clear.

## 3. Category Trends

- Momentum categories
  - Eyeshadow: bold palettes with multichrome/duochrome shimmers; smoky silvers; jewel‑tone greens/teals; pink monochromes for seasonal content.
  - Lips: glossy, high‑shine toppers over dark liner; parallel rise of blurred, comfy liquid mattes.
  - Blush: cream‑to‑powder layering; luminous baked drugstore (Covergirl) and jelly textures (Lethal Jelly Sticks).
  - Setting/finishing: pressed powders and finishing powders to tame glow; reformulation wins (Huda pressed).
  - Eye toppers/glitter: chunky glitter gels (Lethal X‑Ray) and liquid shimmers for wet‑look lids.

- Aesthetics/textures

  - “Spicy neutrals”: cool‑toned and muted color stories functioning as everyday wear.

- Fatigue/skepticism

- Seasonal shift
  - Pink/berry monochrome eye stories ramping (Valentine’s cadence).
  - Continued appetite for greens/teals despite winter timing.

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- Licensed IP drives indie sell‑through: Lethal x SpongeBob appeared across creators with consistent quality praise and urgency messaging (EU indie momentum).
- High‑impact shimmers remain the purchase trigger: creators repeatedly center lid impact and shift as the deciding factor (Nomad, Lethal, Give Me Glow).
- Alternative/glam‑goth aesthetics normalize as “wearable”: smoky cool mattes + silver shimmers; “spicy neutrals” redefine everyday palettes (US indie: Blend Bunny, Nomad).
- Lip texture bifurcation: dark liner + glassy gloss (indie/C‑beauty Kaleidos) coexists with blurred, non‑drying liquids (ColourPop), suggesting broader matte comeback under comfort framing.
- Blush draping and layering is standard practice: cream base set with powder for saturation and longevity (Rare Beauty + indie powders).
- Reformulation redemption arc: Armani Luminous Silk and Huda pressed powder overcome initial skepticism; “improved texture without heaviness” is resonating.
- Regional momentum
  - EU indie: Lethal (DE) gaining visibility via IP collab and thematic accessories.
  - US indie: Nomad, Blend Bunny, Unearthly dominate palette/cheek chatter.
  - C‑beauty: Kaleidos lip finishes are the preferred gloss/glaze toppers.
  - Drugstore US: Covergirl luminous baked face line benefiting from “Hourglass dupe” talk.

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- Stock/feature now
  - Multichrome singles and liquid toppers: prioritize reliable, non‑flaky textures; bundle with glitter primer/adhesive.
  - “Spicy neutral” palettes: cool‑leaning, muted color stories with strong shimmers (seek distribution with US indie analogs if Nomad not wholesale).
  - Blush layering kits: pair cream (jelly/bouncy or liquid) with complementary powder; include draping placement guides.
  - Lip combo bundles: deep liner (Black Moon‑style shades) + high‑shine glaze/oil (Kaleidos‑like finish); offer curated shade duos.
  - Luminous baked face powders (drugstore‑adjacent price tier): capitalize on “Hourglass dupe” demand with indie/private label options that are pigmented but non‑glittery.
  - Colored/tubing mascaras and waterproof gel liners (burgundy, ruby, brown): small but buzzed add‑ons.

- Prepare for near‑term demand
  - Pink/berry monochrome eye stories (Valentine’s): stock pink palettes, sparkly toppers, pink‑leaning eye bases.
  - Pre‑order/waitlist: set notifications for Starborn Nascent; communicate first‑drop allocations to capture collector traffic.
  - EU indie accessories: themed bags/blotting papers/brush sets akin to Lethal’s drop—seek similar novelty items from suppliers.

- Merchandising and education
  - “Glam Goth Smoky Kit”: cool mattes + silver/icy multichrome + liner.
  - “No‑Dry Matte Lip Set”: blurred liquid lip + hydrating prep + matching liner.
  - Include glitter primer automatically with any flaky‑prone shimmer purchases; message why (fallout control).

- What to avoid or caveat
  - Weak‑hold brow gels: steer to “hurricane hold” options; clearly label hold levels.
  - Flaky shimmer formulas without guidance: if stocked, bundle with adhesive and set expectations in copy.
  - Face palettes with limited shade ranges: avoid single‑shade face kits; offer multiple depth options or custom bundles.

- Regional sourcing strategy
  - Balance EU indie (Lethal‑like complexity) with US indie staples (Blend Bunny/Unearthly equivalents) and C‑beauty gloss finishes (Kaleidos‑style) to reflect creator usage patterns.
  - Highlight vegan/cruelty‑free badges; creators repeatedly surfaced this as a plus.

Overall: Lean into high‑impact eye textures, lip layering systems, and blush duos. Anchor with urgency (LE, pre‑launch), value (dupe framing), and performance education to convert the current wave of indie‑driven enthusiasm.
