# Weekly Top 10 Indie Makeup Product Mentions

**Reporting Period:** January 12–18, 2026

1.  **Pinky Promise Palette by Bella Beauté Bar x DeAndra Nicole** — 16 mentions  
    This palette dominated mentions due to its highly anticipated launch as a collaboration with creator DeAndra Nicole. Creators celebrated its vibrant, all-pink monochromatic color story, featuring high-quality mattes and impactful multi-chrome shimmers. A key theme was its intentional design for deeper skin tones, with the collaborator noting it provides the saturation and depth often missing in other palettes.

2.  **Nascent Palette by Starborn Cosmetics** — 10 mentions  
    This palette generated significant buzz as the debut launch from Starborn Cosmetics, a new brand by creator @badtothebrow. Creators highlighted its impressive performance, noting the highly saturated mattes and unique, drier-formula shimmers that provide intense sparkle without creasing. The versatile color story was used to create vibrant eye looks, and creators also demonstrated its use as a face highlighter and lip topper.

3.  **Jelly Sticks by Lethal Cosmetics** — 4 mentions  
    Featured as part of the limited edition SpongeBob collection, these cheek products were praised for their innovative format and performance. Creators loved the bouncy, cooling texture of the jelly stick, noting the blush shades provided a hydrating, glowy finish. The highlighter was also commended for its smooth application, with the overall sentiment being that the product line was unique and fun to use.

4.  **Lip Liners by Black Moon Cosmetics** — 4 mentions  
    These lip liners were consistently featured as a foundational step for creating defined, alternative lip looks. Creators used the dark, neutral shades to provide depth and structure to the lips before applying gloss or lipstick. The formula was praised across mentions for being smooth, highly pigmented, weightless, and long-lasting once set.

5.  **Paris La Bohème Palette by Nomad Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    This new palette from Nomad Cosmetics received strong positive reactions from creators who received it in PR. It was praised for its versatility and performance, with creators successfully executing a range of distinct, highly pigmented eye looks. The formula was noted for being blendable and impactful, while the palette's thematic packaging also drew positive comments.

6.  **16 Hours Later Setting Spray by Lethal Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    This setting spray, featured in the SpongeBob collaboration, was highlighted as both a fun collection piece and a reliable standalone product. Creators praised its ability to seamlessly mesh makeup and extend wear, with one user referring to it as an "iconic" offering from the brand. Its fine mist and long-wear claims were key positive attributes mentioned.

7.  **SpongeBob Lip Gloss by Lethal Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    As a component of the popular SpongeBob collection, these lip glosses were praised for their comfortable and high-shine formula. Creators described the texture as non-goopy and lightweight, highlighting the beautiful sparkly finish. The sentiment was very positive, focusing on both the fun theme and the quality of the gloss itself.

8.  **Bleeding Hearts Palette by Gourmande Girls Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    This upcoming palette was teased by the brand, generating interest with its "anti-Valentine," gothic glam theme. Macro photography revealed a unique mix of special shades, including high-impact holographic glitters and deep, shifty dark tones. The palette is positioned as a dark, spellbinding release for those who prefer a spooky aesthetic over traditional romantic themes.

9.  **Made a Monster Palette by Unearthly Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    This palette was showcased in the creation of highly artistic and alternative makeup looks. A creator used its shades to execute a complex, galaxy-themed face paint look, demonstrating its strong pigmentation and impactful shimmers. The brand also highlighted the palette's sparkly, high-impact shimmer formulas in swatch posts.

10. **Cloud Whip Liquid Lipstick by ColourPop** — 3 mentions  
    This new liquid lipstick formula from ColourPop was highly praised for its comfortable and innovative texture. Creators loved its non-drying, lightweight, and velvety feel, which provides a soft, blurring finish unlike typical matte liquid lipsticks. Its strong performance, comfortable wear, and affordable price point were recurring themes in the positive reviews.
