# Weekly Top 10 Indie Makeup Product Mentions

**Reporting Period:** April 20–26, 2026

1. **Greek Odyssey Palette by Nomad Cosmetics** — 5 mentions  
   Creators praised this palette for its unique blue, teal, and antique gold color story inspired by Greek mythology. The formula was highly rated for its blendability, high-pigment mattes, and crease-proof shimmers that easily layer to create complex, multi-dimensional looks.

2. **Rose Metal Palette by Shine By SD Cosmetics** — 5 mentions  
   This highly anticipated collaboration with creator Deandra Nicole features a five-pan collection of high-shine, foil-like metallic singles. Creators highlighted the unique juxtaposition of organic and industrial themes, specifically praising the intense color payoff and the teal-to-silver duochrome shifts.

3. **Pastel Perihelion Collection by Terra Moons Cosmetics** — 5 mentions  
   Creators showcased this collection for its smooth, non-flaky pastel shimmers and impressive duochrome shifts. The shades were frequently used to add ethereal, light-catching inner corner highlights and center-lid sparkles to both simple and editorial eye looks.

4. **Euphoria Collection by Half Magic Beauty** — 5 mentions  
   Highly praised for its deep indigo packaging and multidimensional sparkle, this collaboration was a favorite for creating winged, glitter-heavy editorial looks. Creators specifically highlighted the "Feel Something" Glitterpuck for its purple-leaning blue shift and the Magic Flik liner for sharp graphic wings.

5. **Skyfire Palette by What's Up Beauty** — 4 mentions  
   Creators were enthusiastic about this 12-pan palette's smooth, talc-free mattes and incredibly sparkly, tackier shimmers that do not crease. The color story, featuring pink and red mattes paired with vibrant duochromes, was recommended for both office-friendly and wet-looking colorful styles.

6. **Super Shock Highlighters by ColourPop** — 4 mentions  
   These cream-to-powder highlighters were celebrated for their metallic, multidimensional finishes and nostalgic appeal. While creators loved the intense pigmentation and smooth texture, some noted that lighter shades can leave a white cast on medium-to-deep skin tones.

7. **Barely Blushing / Barely Alive Powder by Melt Cosmetics** — 4 mentions  
   This setting powder serves as a reliable, go-to staple for multiple creators who use it to set areas prone to creasing, such as the under-eyes and T-zone. It is frequently praised for its consistent performance and ability to seamlessly lock in base makeup.

8. **Spectra Stamp/Pencil by Glisten Cosmetics** — 4 mentions  
   Creators utilized these vibrant tools to add playful graphic elements to their looks, such as stamped orange stars and sharp black or blue liner details. The products were highly valued for their precision and ability to elevate creative, alternative makeup aesthetics.

9. **Yin & Yang Palette by Bella Beaute Bar** — 3 mentions  
   Created in collaboration with Freya86, this palette was praised for its cool-toned, grungy gray and purple mattes alongside intense, non-creasing shimmers. Creators also highlighted the innovative inclusion of a split-pan water-activated liner, though some noted a slight learning curve for deeper skin tones.

10. **Illuminating Highlighter Balm by Nomad Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    Part of the Greek Odyssey collection, this highlighter received mixed reviews from creators regarding its application. While the luxurious packaging and pretty pink sparkle were appreciated, the formula was often described as too subtle and stiff, requiring finger application to warm up the product and prevent foundation lifting.
