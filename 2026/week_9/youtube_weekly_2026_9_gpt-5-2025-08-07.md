# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway: For the week of Feb 23–Mar 1, 2026, indie eyeshadow singles and high-shine multichromes surged around the Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin launch, while grunge-leaning lip combos and “blurring, radiant” bronzers shaped purchase intent across creators.

## 1. Overview

The week felt decisively indie-driven, anchored by multi-creator coverage of the Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin Spring Butterfly single-shadow collection. Content skewed highly visual: macro swatches, wet-look shimmers, and green-forward color stories. The narrative was less “dupe drama” and more “quality-per-dollar,” with creators underscoring performance, texture, and customization.

Shorts and fast tutorials pushed alt/grunge lips: blackened liners, olive/brown tones, metallic toppers, and unusual center shades (yellow, teal) layered over deep liners. These clips are short, repeatable, and basket-building—creators explicitly show two-product “recipes” that translate to immediate add-to-cart behavior.

Complexion discussions split: enthusiasm for soft-focus/radiant bronzers (Saie) and practical correction techniques (Manny’s bruise-camouflage, stick concealers). But there’s cautious feedback on base stacking—hydration plus certain foundations caused separation—and mixed reactions to newer stick concealers (Milk) and peel-off lip stains.

Seasonal cues are clear: nature/butterfly/forest motifs, greens and oranges in complementary blocks, and floral/plant palette rankings. Texture talk centered on “wet,” high-shine multichromes over deep mattes; creators highlighted formulas forgiving on textured or mature lids.

## 2. Product Momentum (What’s Being Talked About)

- Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin Spring Butterfly Singles (singles + magnetic palette)
  - Sentiment: strong positive across Heather Austin, Angelica Nyqvist, Battybean, Amy Loves Makeup, Rachel Palmieri.
  - Context: high-impact multichromes, pigmented mattes that remain blendable/forgiving; clear price transparency (mattes $3.90, shimmers $5.90, multichromes $10.90; bundle discount). Peak launch-week hype; heavy cross-creator adoption; robust macro swatches drove urgency.
  - Clusters: “customize your own palette,” “greens done right,” “multichrome shifts on camera,” “texture-friendly.”

- Cosmic Brushes (often misnamed “Cosmic Beauty” this week)
  - Undergrowth palette: repeated top ranking (Battybean #1) for swampy greens/murky yellows; multichrome depth praised.
  - Angelic palette: positive swatch-first reveal (Angelica). Position: rising UK indie comparison point for green/alt palettes.

- Bella Beauté Bar
  - Dead Roses Vol 2, Poison Garden, Secret Garden (plus Dead Roses highlighter): consistently positive for shifty shimmers and murky garden themes. Holds as a reliable “garden shimmer” benchmark.

- Clionadh
  - Ripple shadow and glitter adhesive: high-impact centerpiece shimmer and reliable adhesion. Trend anchor for “wet-look” lids.

- Give Me Glow singles (Mojito, Sea Princess, Limeade)
  - Sentiment: very positive; immediate neon payoff and layering power over dark bases. Cementing as a go-to for bright inner-corner/gradient pops.

- Rokael Beauty Le Diamante Animale Palette
  - Sentiment: strong positive; hybrid cream-powder shimmers called “revolutionary” for wet shine. Early pro-grade hype.

- Saie Super Suede Bronzer
  - Sentiment: positive (Heather Austin; Manny). Noted for blurring + natural radiance; higher pigment than typical baked formulas. Becoming the “clean, radiant bronzer” reference.

- Drugstore face updates
  - Milani baked blush/bronzer/highlighter reformulations: positive (smoother, more pigmented, less glittery).
  - Covergirl baked bronzer: positive value, Hourglass-adjacent finish mention.

- Mixed/negative repeat notes
  - Milk Hydro Grip Gel Stick Concealer: mixed; stiffer texture, lighter-than-claim coverage—brush works better than sponge.
  - MUFE Cosmic lip liner: negative; fragility/breakage and waste.
  - Tree Hut Vanilla Shave Oil: negative scent feedback (alcoholic/chemical).
  - Tirtir cushion: mixed—coverage liked, but shade-matching friction and poor tester conditions called out.

- Lip combos driving baskets
  - Black Moon lip pencils (Exhume, Tabby, Tortie) + Kaleidos lip glaze/clay (Freefall, Cypher), Unearthly Frostbite metallic, Klarity Coffee Steam gloss, Lawless Cinnamon liner. Visual “before/after” toppers and two-product ombrés dominated shorts.

- Product combinations creators tested
  - Deep matte bases + multichrome toppers with glitter adhesive (Clionadh/Oden’s Eye/Unearthly/NYX).
  - Cross-brand shadow stacks (Beauty Bay mattes + De(ver)une toppers; Chokehold darks + Give Me Glow neons).
  - Lip: deep liner base + center metallic/gloss/clay for dimensional ombré.

## 3. Category Trends

- Eyeshadow
  - Momentum: very high for singles and multichromes; customization over pre-made; greens, oranges, purples. “Wet” shimmer over deep, smoky mattes; adhesives are essentials.
  - Aesthetics: butterfly/forest/floral motifs; green–orange complementary blocking; “grungy pastels.”
  - Fatigue: light skepticism that some black-based multichromes feel common—but still desirable for payoff.

- Lips
  - Momentum: alt/grunge combinations; olive/teal/yellow centers; metallic and glossy toppers over deep liners.
  - Clear shift to “recipe content” that normalizes unconventional shades via brown bases.

- Complexion
  - Blurring, radiant bronzers trending; soft-focus language resonates. Stick/concealer formats see mixed reactions—texture and blend method matter.
  - Color correction re-surfaced via bruise coverage (orange correctors; underpainting; longevity stacks).

- Cheeks
  - Baked, luminous but refined finishes (Milani, Covergirl) gaining; cool pinks visible in drugstore haul content.

- Mascara/Eyeliner
  - Tubing mascara lift (Urban Decay Tube Job) and creamy angled gel sticks; affordability vs performance dialogue continues (NYX Inky Stix praised).

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- Indie singles > palettes
  - Cross-creator embrace of Oden’s Eye singles and magnetic palettes; “pick what you’ll use” value story. Singles as a springboard for greens and multichrome play.

- Nature-coded color stories
  - Butterflies, forest/moss, florals across Oden’s Eye, Cosmic Brushes, Bella Beauté Bar, Lady Bug Glow. Greens are the hero; oranges as complementary heat.

- Texture-first validation
  - “Wet-look” multichromes over deep mattes; adhesives as standard step; formulas praised for being forgiving on texture/mature lids (explicitly noted by multiple creators).

- Alt/grunge lips as a repeatable format
  - Multiple creators demonstrate liner + center shade recipes to “wearabilize” unusual tones (olive, teal, yellow), pushing liner/gloss/clay attachment sales.

- Value and reformulations
  - Drugstore baked face products framed as luxury-adjacent; reformulations highlighted (Milani). Dupe thinking persists (NYX/Huda powder narratives) but is less central than texture/performance.

- Regional signals and brand-origin momentum
  - Western indie dominance (Oden’s Eye [Sweden], Cosmic Brushes [UK], Bella Beauté Bar, Give Me Glow, Black Moon).
  - C-beauty lip success (Kaleidos) for blendable, saturated alterna-shades.
  - K-beauty complexion (Tirtir) enters mainstream retail conversation; demand tempered by shade-selection friction.
  - Additional indie visibility with possible LatAm influence cues (Lady Bug Glow’s Portuguese naming and winter-forest theme).

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- High intent
  - Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin singles: multi-creator coverage, clear pricing, bundle discount, heavy macro swatches, and claims of texture-friendliness. Expect fast sell-through on standout greens (Lily Stem, Ancient Forest), high-shift shimmers (Nectar, Aurora), and core deep matte (Broken Branches).
  - Saie Super Suede bronzer: repeated “blurry/naturally radiant” language; favorable comparisons vs baked norms.
  - Rokael Beauty Le Diamante: unique hybrid texture positioned as pro-grade; early adopter buzz.
  - Give Me Glow neons and Clionadh shimmers: “one-swipe,” immediate payoff in shorts drives impulse buys.

- Solid everyday/value drivers
  - Milani baked cheek line (improved texture/pigment), Covergirl baked bronzer (Hourglass-adjacent finish), Urban Decay tubing mascara (lift/length on straight lashes).

- Cautionary signals
  - MUFE Cosmic lip liner: fragility/breakage—avoid or clearly warn.
  - Milk Hydro Grip Stick Concealer: technique- and tool-dependent; set expectations (brush over sponge; lighter coverage).
  - Tirtir cushion: shade-match barriers—reduce returns through shade tools/samples.
  - Tree Hut Vanilla Shave Oil: negative scent feedback; likely underperformer for scent-driven shoppers.
  - Peel-off lip liner stain (Hard Candy): messy/uncomfortable—low repeat purchase likelihood.

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- Stock/expand
  - High-shift singles and multichromes with robust supply: consider Clionadh, Karla, Terra Moons, Adept singles; pair with:
    - Empty magnetic palettes (15-pan option) and tools to build custom quads/bundles.
    - Glitter primers/adhesives (Clionadh, Unearthly, NYX) and texture-friendly eye bases (DeDe Signature–style).
  - Green–orange curation
    - Create “Spring Forest” kits: deep matte greens + neon/lime + copper/orange shimmers + adhesive.
  - Alt/grunge lip hub
    - Deep liners (black, grey-brown, espresso), olive/teal/metallic bullets and liquid lips, high-shine toppers. Showcase “lip recipes” on PDPs to lift attachment rate.
    - Spotlight C-beauty Kaleidos lip clays/glazes if accessible; if not, seek comparable indie textures.
  - Blurring, radiant bronzers
    - If Saie not wholesale-accessible, identify indie analogues with soft-focus micro-fine pearl; merchandise as “blurring bronzer” with demo video.

- Merchandising/messaging
  - “Forgiving on texture” callouts for shimmers/mattes; macro swatches under shifting light.
  - Bundle pricing mirroring Oden’s Eye’s value cue (e.g., 10–15% off singles set + free magnetic palette).
  - Education content: short reels replicating creator techniques—deep matte base + wet multichrome topper; lip ombrés over brown liner.

- Fill gaps highlighted by creators
  - Reliable lip liners (avoid fragile formulas), long-wear non-peel lip stains/balms, and texture-friendly stick concealers (with tool guidance).
  - Shade-matching aids for cushions/concealers: undertone charts, swatch comparisons, low-cost sample cards to mitigate returns.

- De-emphasize/avoid
  - Peel-off lip liner stains and alcohol-leaning shave oils unless formulas test well in-house.
  - Fragile pencil formats with breakage complaints.

- Timing
  - Ride the Oden’s Eye halo with parallel indie singles curated drops now; lead with greens and high-shift neutrals. Refresh alt lip recipes weekly to sustain short-form traffic and repeat purchases.
