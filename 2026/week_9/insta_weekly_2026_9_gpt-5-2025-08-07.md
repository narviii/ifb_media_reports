# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key Takeaway: Feb 23–Mar 1, 2026 delivered a cool-toned, sparkle-first week: icy chromes, chunky glitters, and metallic lips led feed aesthetics, while indie launches from Dieverune, Unearthly, and Oden’s Eye fueled both purchase intent and pricing debates.

## 1. Overview

- The Vibe:
  - Heavy on high-shine demos and swatch reels: multichrome single roundups, “ethereal green” edits, and collection overviews outperformed static photos.
  - Tutorials skewed practical (brush pressure, color-correcting, crease placement) with short, visual proofs.
  - Aesthetic-forward GRWMs and lip-combo shorts spiked virality (frosted/cool-toned lips, metallic gold/silver foils).

- Engagement Temperature:
  - Sparkly singles and chrome palettes = high excitement + “send me this post” saves.
  - Clear buy signals for cool-toned glosses, icy highlighters, and jewel-toned multichromes.
  - Notable friction points: European availability (Half Magic), product behavior (glitter puck shrinkage), and price hikes/pan-size confusion (Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin).

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

- Top Mentioned Brands:
  - Dieverune, Unearthly Cosmetics, Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin, Half Magic, Glisten Cosmetics, Clionadh, Terra Moons, Dramo (C-Beauty), Wet n Wild (new multichromes/cheeks), plus steady mentions of MAC, Rituel de Fille, ColourPop.

- Viral Products:
  - Dieverune
    - Silver and Cold collection (palette + singles like Bismuth/Sterling), icy highlighters, colored mascaras; Gloomy Glosses (Abscission/Hoarfrost) and Leaden lip mud (soft blue-grey). Comments include “spending a BAGGGG” and wishlists; EU stock via Monolith teased.
  - Unearthly Cosmetics
    - “Bitter” (blue-brown shimmer with red flecks) teased within Woeful Whimsy collab with @s.andromedae; repeated “when’s launch?” demand.
  - Half Magic
    - Glitter Pucks (Princess Ghost) and Glitterpill (Mother Mist): repeated placement on eyes, cheeks, ears; multiple “not available in Europe” laments and one shrinkage complaint.
  - Oden’s Eye x Heather Austin
    - Spring Butterfly singles launch drove buzz and pushback: excitement for shades (“Ancient Forest,” “Aurora”) vs. comments on price jump/pan compatibility.
  - Chromes & Singles
    - Dramo chrome palettes (multiple reels, strong views/likes), Terra Moons “Heart Nebula,” Clionadh “Vineyard” and deep iridescents (“Ori/Aureate”) surfaced in purchase-intent comments.
  - Lips (Cool/Frost/Blue-Grey)
    - Revlon Skyline Pink (pearl) surged via creator-to-creator influence; Kryolan Faceliners (11/23) used as cool taupe/grey lip liners; Dieverune Leaden lip mud as comfy blue-grey; metallic foils via Glisten Spectra (gold/silver).

- The “Look” Breakdown:
  - Eyes:
    - Dominant textures: multichrome toppers, high-impact sparkly shimmers, chunky creamy glitters (glitterpucks), and icy ethereal greens. Smoky bases (taupe/charcoal) anchor maximal sparkle.
    - Techniques: blown-out shimmer past orbital, graphic “floating” liner, neon accents, bleached/soft brows; under-eye sparkle drift onto upper cheek (editorial).
  - Cheeks:
    - Dewy, light-reflective blushes that survive powdering (Marylia Scott Glush); cool-toned bronzers emerging (Saie SuperSuede test); Wet n Wild Mother Fluffer cheeks gaining mentions.
  - Lips:
    - Cool and experimental: frosted pinks, metallic gold/silver foils, blue/blue-grey bullets/muds; iridescent gloss toppers (pink–blue–violet shifts) finalized most looks.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

- Visual Themes:
  - #gothmakeup/#softgoth sensibility blended with high sparkle.
  - Editorial glitter placement (eyes-to-cheek), bleached/hidden brows, nose/cheek stamps, graphic cat motifs.
  - “Alt luxury” cues: piercings, spiked septums, chain jewelry alongside luminous skin.

- Color Stories:
  - Cool continuum: icy silver/steel, indigo/navy, slate, with teal/cyan flips.
  - Ethereal/mossy greens (mint–chartreuse–olive), often duochrome with pink/gold shifts.
  - Blue-brown classics updated with cooler, less red bases; neon accents (lime/chartreuse) as liners.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

- Stocking Recommendations:
  - Cool-toned sparkly ecosystem
    - Singles: Dieverune (Bismuth/Sterling + icy highlighters), Terra Moons “Heart Nebula,” Clionadh “Vineyard,” deep iridescents (“Ori/Aureate”), Wet n Wild jeweled multichromes (value hook).
    - Palette: Dramo chrome palettes (strong aesthetic pull); Dieverune Silver and Cold (if available wholesale).
  - Lip zone for cool/frost/metallic:
    - Revlon Skyline Pink (pearl), comfortable blue-grey bullets/muds, cool taupe/grey liners (Kryolan 11/23 analogues), liquid metal bases (Glisten Spectra-style).
  - Texture toppers/accessories:
    - Chunky creamy glitters (Half Magic-like), chrome flakie gels, colored mascaras (navy/grey/lilac), face-safe gems.

- Content Strategy:
  - Short, swatch-first reels grouping “tones” (mossy greens, ethereal mints, blue-browns) convert—mirror Smita’s format with honest texture notes (emollient vs. set).
  - Quick tips with proof (brush pressure, color-correct placement) drive saves—translate to bite-size clips.
  - Lip cocktails: pair cool liners + muted bullets + iridescent gloss; include “on teeth” transfer checks and wear notes.
  - Address EU availability up front with alternates/stocking partners; pin shipping/stock FAQs on product pages.

- Gap Analysis:
  - Packaging friction: audience disdain for wasteful/cheap-feel luxury—prioritize refillable/recyclable messaging.
  - Glitter puck shrinkage: educate on storage, offer replacement policies/FAQ; consider gel-cream formats less prone to moisture loss.
  - Green corrector mismatch: curate yellow/olive correctors across shade depths; create visual guide vs. pastel greens.

## 6. Special insights

- Emerging Micro-Trends:
  - Full-on metallic lips (true foil gold/silver) moving from editorial to everyday short-form; expect broader shade expansion (gunmetal, pewter, icy blue).
  - Blue-brown 2.0: cooler, less red bases with micro red flecks (Unearthly “Bitter”)—a modernized club classic.
  - Colored mascaras (navy/grey/lilac) as a low-commitment entry to cool-toned looks.

- Unexpected Pairings:
  - Glitter pucks and chrome bases on ears as “jewelry”; spectra bases used as lip paint; faceliners as lip liners; eyeshadows as blush (blue blush teaser via Woeful Whimsy).

- Creative insight:
  - Build “Cool-Toned Lab” bundles: pick-3 singles (mossy green + icy silver + blue-brown), a cool lip system (grey-taupe liner + pearl frost + iridescent gloss), and a topper (flake/gel). Add a mini guide: storage (to prevent shrinkage), crease-proofing (base choice), and brush-pressure diagrams. This meets the week’s desire lines—sparkle-forward, technique-supported, and cool-leaning—while alleviating known pain points (texture behavior, availability, sustainability).
