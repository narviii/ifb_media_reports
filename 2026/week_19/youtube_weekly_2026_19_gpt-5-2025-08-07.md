# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway: For the week of 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-10, indie YouTube was dominated by cool‑toned grunge eyes, grey/greige lips, and texture-stacked shimmer looks, while creators shifted bases to sheer, heat‑friendly skin. The Bella Beauté Bar x Freya collab led buzz; Oden’s Eye and texture toppers kept momentum; value highlighters and speed-focused tools signaled practical buying.

## 1. Overview

This week felt alt-forward and texture-obsessed. Multiple creators layered primers, metallics, and flakes to build “wet” and multichrome payoff, with little skepticism about performance.

Complexion moved lighter and faster: sunscreen primers, skin tints, and car-application routines emphasized speed and reliability in heat. Blush leaned sunburned/sun‑kissed and pink powders reappeared for brightening.

Shade narratives skewed cool: greys, greige-browns, navy/blue smokes, and pink‑lilac shifts. Grey lip tones weren’t a novelty—they read as daily-wear for alt viewers.

Value came up via Revolution’s highlighter trio (“bargain glow”) and tool ROI (BK Beauty’s N18 cited for speed). Nostalgia/throwbacks (Oden’s Eye highlighter; discontinued Colourpop Seismic) quietly fueled dupe-hunting.

Affiliate-code usage and repeat appearances of the same collab (Bella Beauté Bar x Freya) indicate coordinated, purchase-ready attention rather than casual mention.

## 3. Category Trends

- Eyes
  - Momentum: cool-toned palettes (greys, navy, teal), high-shine metallics, multichrome/flake toppers.
  - Technique: exaggerated outer corners, smoky lower lash, shimmer bases to intensify payoff.

- Lips
  - Momentum: grey/greige, black, deep wine, metallic blue; liners as structural anchors.
  - Finish: matte or mousse-y with coordinating gloss toppers.

- Complexion
  - Shift to sheer/glowy bases (skin tints, CC), sun‑kissed/sunburned blush placement, pink brightening powders.
  - Baked/soft-focus finishing powders keep HG status.

- Highlighter
  - Iridescent pink‑lilac shifts, “wet-look” finishes; attention split between indie keepsakes and drugstore value plays.

- Tools
  - Precision/speed brush sets earn coverage; small pencil brushes praised for softness; speed claims resonate.

- Fatigue/skepticism
  - Minimal. Minor tool caveats only (BK N19 feel; N20 with thicker formulas).

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- Cool-toned grunge revival is mainstream within indie channels
  - Repeated use of grey/greige mattes, navy/teal smokes, silvery shimmers (Battybean; Oden’s Eye segments; Bella Beauté Bar collab features).

- Texture stacking as standard workflow
  - Shimmer primers + metallics + flakes/toppers appear across multiple looks (Gavissi, The Pastel Roses, Oden’s Eye, Bella Beauté Bar).

- Summer practicality without sacrificing drama
  - Sheer bases and sunscreen primers (Amy; Battybean in Florida heat), paired with bold eyes/lips.

- Value-conscious glow
  - Explicit bargain framing around Revolution Highlighter Icon; creators still chasing wet, iridescent finishes via accessible options.

- Regional/brand-origin signals
  - Western indie collabs (US) are leading buzz (Bella Beauté Bar x Freya).
  - Scandinavian indie (Oden’s Eye, Sweden) maintains high visibility and affection.
  - UK indie/suppliers (The Pastel Roses; Revolution as UK drugstore) present in both texture and value lanes.
  - C‑beauty (Kaleidos) earns credibility for long-wear lips.
  - K‑beauty lip tints (Laneige) appear as easy add-ons, not centerpieces.

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- High-conversion cues
  - Multiple posts and formats around one collab (Bella Beauté Bar x Freya) with affiliate CTAs.
  - Visual proof: intense sparkle in sunlight; full-coverage black lips; throwback “still holds up” highlighters.
  - “Holy grail” language (Melt Finishing Powder; NARS Soft Matte Concealer).
  - Tool ROI: “speeds me up” brushes (BK N18) in a 15-minute routine.
  - Value framing: “bargain glow” Revolution triad.

- Early/ongoing hype
  - Grey/greige lip tones: distinct swatches plus repeated wear suggest durable demand in alt segments.
  - Texture ecosystem (primer + shimmer + flakes): creators show them as a cohesive system, pushing bundle purchases.

- Negative purchase brakes (light)
  - Mild brush comfort concerns (BK N19), technique caveat for N20 with thick bases—suggest positioning guidance rather than avoidance.

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- Stocking priorities
  - Cool-toned grunge palettes with silvery/high-shine metallics. If Bella Beauté Bar x Freya is unavailable, secure close analogs (cool greys, navy/teal, split-pan or high-sparkle shimmers).
  - Grey/greige/black lip assortment:
    - Liners: deep browns and greys (e.g., Black Moon‑style profiles).
    - Liquids and mousse textures in grey/greige/black; pair with coordinating gloss toppers.
  - Texture ecosystem:
    - Shimmer/Glitter primers (e.g., Oden’s Eye/Bella Beauté Bar equivalents).
    - High-sparkle toppers and flakes (The Pastel Roses‑type multichromes; Gavissi‑style toppers).
  - Wet-look, iridescent highlighters with pink‑lilac shifts; include an affordable value tier to answer the Revolution signal.
  - Speed tools bundle anchored by a standout bronzer/blush brush and soft pencil brush; position explicitly for “10–15 minute glam.”

- Merchandising and bundles
  - “Alt Grunge Eye Kit”: cool-toned palette + shimmer primer + silver topper + pencil brush.
  - “Grey Lip System”: grey/greige liner + mousse/liq lip + coordinating gloss.
  - “Celestial Sparkle Stack”: duochrome highlighter + multichrome flakes + shimmer base.
  - “Heat-Proof Face Edit”: sunscreen-friendly primer + skin tint + pink brightening powder + finishing powder.

- Content and PDP strategy
  - Replicate creator combos in how-to visuals and short reels; emphasize opacity tests on challenging shades (e.g., black lips) and sparkle in natural light.
  - Include “use with” guidance (which primer to pair with which shimmer/toppers).
  - Create a “Cool-Tone Finder” filter for palettes and lip shades; surface greys/greiges first.

- Sourcing and brand mix
  - Maintain/expand Scandinavian indie (Oden’s Eye–like offerings) and UK texture suppliers (The Pastel Roses analogs).
  - Add at least one C‑beauty long-wear lip line (Kaleidos‑adjacent) to address durability demand.
  - Keep a small K‑beauty lip tint slot as an upsell add-on.

- Risk management
  - If carrying BK-type tools, note usage tips (N20 best with thin bases) and offer softer alternatives for under‑eye setting to preempt comfort complaints.
  - Monitor discontinued cult items (e.g., Colourpop Seismic) and promote closest in‑stock dupes to capture nostalgia-driven searches.

- Gaps to fill
  - Consistent supply of grey/greige lip liners (multiple undertones: lavender‑grey, sage‑greige, taupe‑grey).
  - High‑impact silver shimmers that function both as inner-corner and lid toppers without fallout.
  - Pink brightening powders across skin tones (cool and neutral options) to support the pink‑powder trend.
