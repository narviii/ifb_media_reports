# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

*   **Key Takeaway:** For the week ending May 24, 2026, the indie beauty landscape was dominated by a "Whimsical Goth" aesthetic, merging cool-toned grunge palettes with ethereal, high-shine multichromes. New launches from indie brands like Epic Beauty ("Jewels of Eternity") generated significant pre-launch buzz, while audiences demonstrated strong purchase intent for unique textures and unconventional colors, particularly from KIKO Milano and Rituel de Fille.

## 1. Overview

*   **The Vibe:** The feed was heavily populated with in-depth video tutorials and swatch sessions. Creators focused on demonstrating product performance, especially for new palette launches and high-impact single shadows. The dominant mood was dark and moody but punctuated with celestial, iridescent sparkle, moving beyond simple matte grunge looks.

*   **Engagement Temperature:** Audience engagement was high and specific. Comments moved past generic compliments to direct questions about product formulas ("How do these colors compare?"), application techniques ("What do you use for eyeshadow base/primer?"), and availability ("these are sold out and im so irritated lol"). This indicates a highly educated consumer base actively researching before purchasing.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

*   **Top Mentioned Brands:**
    *   **Rituel de Fille:** Heavily featured across multiple creators due to their Memorial Day sale and new lipstick formula.
    *   **Glisten Cosmetics:** Prominent for their unconventional lip tints and glosses (e.g., grey shades) and the new Bliss 2.0 palette.
    *   **Half Magic Beauty:** Consistently used for glitter and graphic liner elements in creative looks.
    *   **Terra Moons Cosmetics & Clionadh Cosmetics:** The go-to brands for the high-impact multichrome single shadows that defined the week's sparkly aesthetic.
    *   **Epic Beauty:** A new brand from the Monolith Shop EU that generated significant coordinated buzz for its upcoming "Jewels of Eternity" palette.

*   **Viral Products:**
    *   **Epic Beauty "Jewels of Eternity" Palette:** This new, unreleased palette was the talk of the feed. Multiple creators showcased its moody, purple-and-green color story, creating strong pre-launch hype.
    *   **KIKO Milano Water Eyeshadows:** Posts by @glamsmitty featuring the shades "Blue Me Away" and "Lava Vision" drove significant interest, with comments highlighting that the products are now sold out, confirming viral demand.
    *   **Rituel de Fille "Light as a Feather" Lipsticks:** The new formula received positive feedback for its lightweight yet pigmented feel, especially in dark, cool-toned shades like "Black Locust."
    *   **Makeup Forever Artist Liquid Color:** The launch of unconventional shades like "Cobalt Blue" as a multi-use product for lips and cheeks created a viral moment, with high engagement on @ghoul.in.japan's video.

*   **The "Look" Breakdown:**
    *   **EYES:** A strong shift towards cool-toned palettes featuring deep purples, mossy greens, and slate greys. The application technique of choice was layering a high-impact, wet-look duochrome or multichrome shimmer over a smoky matte base.
    *   **LIPS:** A major trend was the "grunge gloss," featuring deep, unconventional colors like grey, taupe, and blackened plum in a high-shine finish. The technique of using a dark liner with a lighter, shimmery center to create dimension was prevalent.
    *   **CHEEKS:** Unconventional blush was a key theme, with creators using lavender, mauve, and even blue shades. Highlighters were not subtle; the preference was for iridescent, duochrome formulas with a distinct blue or violet shift to complement the cool-toned eye looks.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

*   **Visual Themes:** The dominant aesthetic was **#gothmakeup** and **#grungemakeup**, but with an ethereal, whimsical twist. This wasn't the flat, matte grunge of the past; it was defined by iridescent shimmers, scattered facial gems, and a "wet" or "glassy" finish on the skin and eyes.
*   **Color Stories:** Cool tones reigned supreme. The most used palettes revolved around **deep purples, forest greens, and icy blues**. These were often paired with a contrasting **cool-toned lavender or pink blush**, creating a cohesive, otherworldly look.

## 4. Purchase-Intent Signals (From Comments)

*   **"Add to Cart" Energy:** Direct statements of intent were common. On the CheerYep palettes post: "Thats stunning!! Definitely adding these to my collection!!" and "I need ember right meow 😍". For Blend Bunny: "I sooo want to get her palettes! ALL OF THEM!".
*   **Restock Demands:** The KIKO Milano chrome shadows are a clear example of demand outstripping supply. A comment on @glamsmitty's post reads, "these are sold out and im so irritated lol," signaling a missed sales opportunity for those who can't find them.
*   **Curiosity Clusters:**
    *   **Primers:** Multiple users asked about eyeshadow primers, indicating a need for content and products that ensure the longevity of these complex, layered eye looks. Robert Welsh's video on using MAC Paint Pot for nose foundation also drove primer-related questions.
    *   **Formula & Dupes:** Followers are asking for comparisons between products (r.e.m. beauty palettes, Dior vs. Haus Labs highlighters), showing they are making strategic purchasing decisions.
    *   **Unconventional Use:** The comment "running to put sparkle sticks on my lips" on a lip combo video shows audiences are inspired by and willing to try multi-use techniques.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

*   **Stocking Recommendations:**
    *   Prioritize cool-toned eyeshadow palettes with a focus on purples, greens, and greys. The "Jewels of Eternity" palette is a perfect example of the trending color story.
    *   Invest heavily in single shadows with duochrome and multichrome finishes. Brands like Terra Moons are essential.
    *   Expand lip offerings to include unconventional gloss and lipstick shades: greys, taupes, deep purples, and blackened berries.
    *   Stock iridescent highlighters with blue, violet, and green shifts (e.g., Rituel de Fille Apparition, Bella Beaute Bar Skin of a Killer).

*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   Video content is king. Produce reels that demonstrate **wet vs. dry application** of shimmers, as this was a popular and effective format.
    *   Create tutorials focused on achieving the "Whimsical Goth" look, breaking down how to layer mattes and shimmers for maximum impact.
    *   Address common audience questions directly. A video on "The Best Eyeshadow Primers for Foiled Shadows" would perform well.

*   **Gap Analysis:** The negative feedback on the MAC Film Noir blush reformulation ("ugh why do they keep taking away the more pigmented versions") reveals a market gap. Customers want deep, richly pigmented blushes that are buildable and don't appear ashy. This is an opportunity to highlight existing products or source new ones that meet this specific need.

## 6. Special insights

*   **Emerging Micro-Trends:**
    *   **"Watercolor" Cheeks:** The description of the Makeup Forever liquid colors as feeling like "watercolor on your face" points to a desire for thin, serum-like, buildable formulas that can deliver intense, unconventional color without heavy texture.
    *   **Eyeshadow as Lip Topper:** Creators are using jelly or glitter eyeshadows as a final step in their lip combos to add unique dimension and sparkle. This DIY approach signals an interest in multi-use products and customizable lip finishes.
    *   **Decorative Face Embellishments:** Beyond simple gems, creators are using scattered pearls (@s.andromedae) and precise star stamps (@courtneyquarantine) to elevate eye looks from makeup to facial art.

*   **Unexpected Pairings:** The core aesthetic of the week is an unexpected pairing: the dark, moody base of grunge makeup is being combined with the bright, ethereal sparkle of fairy/celestial aesthetics. This fusion creates a look that is both edgy and dreamy.

*   **Creative insight:** The influence of C-Beauty brands (@cheeryep_makeup, @joocyee_official, @sugarock_official) is becoming more pronounced. Their unique packaging (e.g., keychain charms on palettes) and innovative textures (e.g., chunky star glitters) are being integrated into Western indie looks, pushing the boundaries of creativity and introducing a more playful, tactile element to the grunge trend. This East-meets-West fusion is a key driver of the "Whimsical Goth" movement.
