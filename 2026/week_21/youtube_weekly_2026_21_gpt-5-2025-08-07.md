# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway: May 18–24, 2026 was shimmer-forward and indie-led: multichrome palettes, glow-first cheeks, and cool-toned lip looks drove attention, while value talk (dupes, cost-per-gram) and EU indie distribution moves signaled near-term purchase spikes.

## 1. Overview

Creators leaned into wet-look and high-shine textures across eyes and cheeks, with multiple shorts showcasing instant-payoff shimmer and “glass” finishes. The vibe was creative and alt-leaning (grunge pastels, gothic romance), but anchored by practical, daily-wear neutrals from Sydney Grace and accessible drugstore base products.

There was clear lip experimentation: cool-toned MLBB stains, boba/ombre lip contours, and sparkle balms. The audience gravitated to easy-to-execute combos that transform basics (cool liner + warm center + clear gloss; peel-off stain + gloss).

Texture discourse sharpened: sparkle vs refined glow. Some creators celebrated ultra-sparkly multichromes; others praised satin metallics for everyday wear and mature lids. Highlighters with visible glitter drew skepticism.

Value and accessibility narratives remained strong: a high-engagement dupe test (e.l.f. vs Givenchy), cost-per-gram critiques at drugstore, and EU-friendly indie distribution (Monolith’s new brand Epic Beauty) all point to price- and logistics-aware purchasing.

Seasonally, bronzed, luminous skin and wearable colored liners surfaced alongside convenience formats (sticks, peel-offs). Inclusivity via dual-depth palettes (Light/Deep) earned positive note.

## 3. Category Trends

- Momentum categories
  - Eyeshadow palettes: indie-first, shimmer-special (multichrome/duochrome), plus neutral/satin options. Customization (magnetic pans) appreciated.
  - Lips: cool-toned stains, grey-taupe liners, ombre/boba contours, gloss toppers, sparkle balms.
  - Cheeks: luminous blushes and blush-highlighter hybrids; cream-powder layering common.
  - Bronzer/face trios: multi-finish cheek kits (cream + shimmer) gaining praise at affordable price points.
  - Eye bases/adhesives: dedicated shimmer primers are now considered essential tools for special shades.

- Texture/aesthetic gravitation
  - Wet-look shimmers, gothic-romance/grunge pastels, and wearable colored liners (burgundy/green matte).
  - Preference split: editorial sparkle vs refined satin shimmer for everyday/mature lids.

- Fatigue/skepticism
  - Visible-glitter highlighters and overly dewy sticks called out as easy to overdo or texturizing.
  - Transfer-prone/thicker lipsticks less favored versus thin, long-wearing or stain-based options.

- Seasonal shift signals
  - Bronzed, luminous skin; stick convenience for summer; MLBB stains + clear gloss; colored yet matte eyeliners for wearable pops.

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- Indie palette wave with regional breadth (cross-creator)
  - UK/EU indie visibility uptick: The Pastel Roses (UK) swatched/used; Epic Beauty (EU) launch via Monolith with global retail partners; Arttitude (UK), Oden’s Eye and Gloss Gods (Sweden) referenced. AU distribution via Glaminatrix.
  - US indie staples maintained: Sydney Grace, Blend Bunny, Bellabeautebar, Fantasy Cosmetica.

- Sparkle vs satin: texture segmentation
  - Alt creators push ultra-sparkly wet-look; swatch-focused coverage amplifies shifts.
  - A parallel demand for satiny metallics persists for daily wear; creators explicitly recommend both lanes.

- Lip trend convergence
  - Cool-toned, naturalistic stains (lip blushing effect) + gloss toppers.
  - 90s/grunge boba lips via cool grey-taupe liners with warmer centers; sparkle balms as statement toppers.

- Value-first discourse
  - Dupe and price-per-gram analysis shaping purchase decisions (e.l.f. vs Givenchy; drugstore powders).
  - Inclusivity as value: dual-depth palettes favorably noted.

- Convenience formats
  - Stick eyeshadows and peel-off lip stains re-enter favor due to speed, low fallout, high payoff.

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- Urgency/exclusivity
  - Singe Beauty Paisley Hoot restock (May 29).
  - New indie brand Epic Beauty (May 31 launch) with EU warehousing and multi-region retail partners; affiliate code windows communicated.

- Combo recipes that sell multiples

- Trust anchors

  - Clear, candid limitations called out (satiny vs hyper-sparkly shimmers; sticky highlight sticks), guiding right-fit purchases.

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- Stock/expand into
  - Shimmer infrastructure: carry Bellabeautebar Shimmer Stay or a high-performing equivalent; merchandize alongside multichrome palettes and singles.
  - Indie shimmer-heavy palettes with distinct color stories: prioritize The Pastel Roses Rose Poetry (UK sourcing) and align early with Epic Beauty (EU) for the Jewels of Eternity launch or first restock. If direct stocking isn’t feasible, curate comparable jewel-toned, multichrome-forward options with magnetic pans.
  - Neutral/satin metallic “office-capable” palettes: add Sydney Grace-style satins/creamy mattes (and ensure Light/Deep depth options) to serve customers avoiding glitter.
  - Lip architecture SKUs:
    - Cool-toned/grey-taupe liners (e.g., Melt Cashmere analogs), warm nude lipsticks that blend well, clear high-shine gloss toppers.
    - Peel-off, cool-toned stains (Sacheu-type) and stain-safe glosses; consider bundling as “MLBB stain kits.”
    - Sparkle balms as statement toppers for the alt/goth segment.
  - Wearable colored eyeliners: matte burgundy/green pens at mid/affordable price points.
  - Multi-finish cheek trios and buildable cream bronzers: prioritize thin, non-sticky textures and mauve/neutral blush options.

- Merchandising plays
  - Bundle builders:
    - “Boba Lip Set” (cool liner + warm center + clear gloss).
    - “Shimmer System” (indie palette + shimmer adhesive + flat shader brush).
    - “Glow, Not Glitter” cheek curation (luminous blushes, refined highlighters).
  - Education content: quick reels on cream + powder blush layering; palette pairing across brands; stain application/peel timing; satin vs sparkly shimmer chooser.

- Where to de-prioritize or message carefully
  - Glitter-forward highlighters and very dewy stick highlighters: either limit assortment or position clearly as editorial, with texture disclaimers.
  - Thick, transfer-prone matte lip bullets: focus on thinner-feel or stain hybrids.

- Market gaps to fill
  - Cool-toned lip assortment breadth (liners, stains, cool nudes) — demand is rising and relatively under-served.
  - Inclusive neutral palettes with depth variants — emphasize Light/Deep skus to mirror Sydney Grace’s positive reception.
  - EU-friendly access to indie multichromes — leverage partners/warehouses to reduce shipping/tax pain points and communicate that clearly on product pages.

- Timing
  - Prepare landing and email for Epic Beauty’s launch window and Singe-adjacent neutral-with-twist restock appetite.
  - Feature ColourPop-like stick shadows analogs if direct stocking isn’t possible; “no-fallout, high-shine” copy and demos convert.

Overall: lean into a two-lane eyeshadow strategy (sparkle-heavy indie + satin/neutral refined), formalize lip-combo kits around cool liners and stains, and attach adhesives/brushes to every shimmer sale.
