# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

*   **Key Takeaway:** Cool-toned blues, purples, and grungy greys dominated the feed, driven by new collection reveals from Gourmande Girls Cosmetics and Shine by SD. The week of March 23-29, 2026, saw a major focus on textural play, with chunky glitters and high-shine multichrome glosses taking center stage, while tutorial reels on unconventional techniques (like using eyeshadow as blush) garnered significant audience engagement.

## 1. Overview

*   **The Vibe:** The week was a dynamic mix of new product hype and educational content. Anticipation built around upcoming "spooky spring" pastel goth palettes, while creators simultaneously deconstructed application techniques in highly-engaging reels. The visual mood was textural and experimental, favoring cool-toned, high-impact looks over simple, natural beauty.

*   **Engagement Temperature:** Audience engagement was highest on practical, technique-driven video content. Reels from Robert Welsh on cream blush application and lip liner technique saw followers expressing gratitude and intent to try the methods. New product reveals from Gourmande Girls and Shine by SD generated significant curiosity, with comments demanding swatches and release dates.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

*   **Top Mentioned Brands:** Glisten Cosmetics, Rituel de Fille, Unearthly Cosmetics, MAC Cosmetics, Kaleidos Makeup, Gourmande Girls Cosmetics, Shine By SD Cosmetics, and Terra Moons Cosmetics were consistently tagged and featured.

*   **Viral Products:**
    *   **Gourmande Girls "Apparition" Palette:** This upcoming "pastel goth" palette was teased and swatched across multiple creator accounts, generating high-intent comments like "Its so cute! I have to have it!"
    *   **Joocyee Lavender/Grey Blush:** A video by creator `ghoul.in.japan` featuring a custom cool-toned lavender blush from C-Beauty brand Joocyee went viral (12k+ likes). The unique "blushtour" (blush + contour) effect sparked intense curiosity in the comments.
    *   **Sushyglow Cosmetics Black Blush:** Another viral moment from `ghoul.in.japan` showcased this true black blush, which was praised for its blendability and versatility as a contour, eyeshadow, and lip ombré product.
    *   **Glisten Cosmetics Juicy Tints:** The shade "Coconut," a unique sheer brown, was featured in a dedicated review by `courtneyquarantine`, driving comments like "wait i need this" and sparking debate on its "spring vampire" aesthetic.
    *   **Kaleidos Highlighters:** A retrospective swatch video by `glamsmitty` reignited interest in the brand's cult-classic highlighters, leading to nostalgic comments and specific demands for the return of discontinued shades.

*   **The "Look" Breakdown:**
    *   **EYES:** A definitive shift to cool tones. Icy blues, deep indigos, lavenders, and mint greens were prevalent, often accented with chunky, iridescent glitter toppers (from Half Magic, Terra Moons) and graphic liners (Glisten Cosmetics).
    *   **LIPS:** A tale of two textures. The feed was split between ultra-glossy, multichrome lips (Unearthly Cosmetics, Glisten Cosmetics) and soft-grunge matte looks, often defined with unconventional navy or grey lip liners (NYX "Foul Mouth").
    *   **CHEEKS:** The standout trend was the repurposing of eyeshadows as blush. Creators used bright purples, greens, and blues from palettes like Unearthly's "Woeful Whimsy" to create unconventional, sculpted cheek looks.

## 3. Aesthetic & Hashtag Trends

*   **Visual Themes:** #gothmakeup and #softgrunge were the dominant aesthetics. This was complemented by a rising #pastelgoth trend, blending spooky elements with lighter spring colors. Content was visually dense, focusing on extreme close-ups of eye textures and glitter.

*   **Color Stories:** Cool-toned palettes reigned supreme. The primary color stories were built around icy blues, lavenders, mint greens, and deep purples. These were often grounded by muted, grungy neutrals like taupe, greige, and smoky navy.

## 4. Purchase-Intent Signals (From Comments)

*   **"Add to Cart" Energy:** Explicit purchase intent was visible across multiple posts. Comments included "I need this ❤️🔥" for Unearthly Cosmetics' "Fractured Halo" lipstick and "Just ordered it" for the new Gourmande Girls palette.

*   **Restock Demands:** The swatch video of classic Kaleidos highlighters prompted a specific call to action from a follower: "I wish they'd re-release the original Mars Melter! I still have it and red highlighters are so rare."

*   **Curiosity Clusters:**
    *   **C-Beauty Cheek Products:** The Joocyee lavender blush generated a flurry of questions ("Link please!!!🙏"), indicating strong interest in sourcing this unique product.
    *   **Unconventional Lip Tints:** Glisten Cosmetics' Juicy Tints prompted specific swatch requests ("I'm DESPERATE to find people swatching Apple"), showing that consumers are actively researching non-traditional shades before purchasing.
    *   **"Makeup Mixology":** A video by `glamsmitty` on mixing an "unwearable" blue Covergirl lipstick with a brown MAC lipstick to create a custom grey-purple shade sparked significant engagement, with followers sharing their own mixing combinations.

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

*   **Stocking Recommendations:**
    *   **Prioritize Cool Tones & Texture:** Stock palettes heavy on blues, purples, and greens. The Gourmande Girls "Apparition" and Shine by SD "Audacious" collections are clear winners. Emphasize chunky glitter toppers and multichrome glosses.
    *   **Invest in "Anti-Blush":** The demand for lavender, grey, and even black blush is a significant, emerging trend. Source these shades or market existing eyeshadows (in larger pans, if possible) for this purpose.
    *   **Expand Grunge Lip Offerings:** Ensure a robust selection of navy, black, deep brown, and grey lip liners to support the soft-grunge lip trend.

*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   **Technique-First Reels:** Create short, impactful tutorials demonstrating how to achieve a specific look (e.g., "How to Use Eyeshadow as Blush," "3 Ways to Wear a Navy Lip Liner"). This format builds trust and drives high engagement.
    *   **Embrace "Makeup Mixology":** Produce content that teaches customers how to mix and layer products they already own to create new shades. This positions the store as an expert resource and encourages creative purchasing.
    *   **Comprehensive Swatch Carousels:** Static image carousels with high-quality, labeled swatches (like those for the Gourmande Girls and Shine by SD launches) are essential for driving pre-launch hype and informing purchase decisions.

*   **Gap Analysis:** A podcast clip from The Double Cleanse Podcast revealed strong negative sentiment towards AI-generated packaging in the indie space, labeling it "cheap." This presents an opportunity to highlight brands with strong artistic direction and human-centric design in marketing copy.

## 6. Special insights

*   **Emerging Micro-Trends:**
    *   **The Lavender/Grey "Blushtour":** The viral Joocyee product signals a niche but growing interest in using cool, grey-toned purples as a combination blush and contour. This creates a sculpted, almost ethereal or "chic corpse" effect that is a fresh take on alternative makeup.
    *   **Curated "Makeup Boxes" as Content:** Creator `ghoul.in.japan`'s video curating a "vampy makeup box" from her collection represents a new content format. It’s not a haul or tutorial, but an act of personal aesthetic curation that resonates deeply with collectors and tells a product story.

*   **Unexpected Pairings:**
    *   **Eyeshadow as Blush:** This was the most prominent multi-use trend of the week. Creator `s.andromedae` explicitly used bright shades from the Unearthly Cosmetics "Woeful Whimsy" palette as blush, demonstrating the versatility of colorful eyeshadow palettes.
    *   **Contour Sticks as Lip Liner:** The KJH.brand Precision Sculpture Stylo was used by `glamsmitty` to line and define lips, proving that face sculpting products can be effectively repurposed.

*   **Creative insight:**
    *   **Subverting "Blush":** The rise of black and grey cheek products signifies a conceptual shift. "Blush" is evolving beyond simulating a "healthy flush" and is now being used to create atmosphere, structure, and even anti-health aesthetics. It's a tool for mood, not just mimicry of vitality. This is a key evolution in the goth and alternative beauty lexicon.
