# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key Takeaway: Mar 23–29, 2026 leaned pastel-goth and chrome: icy blues, chartreuse pops, and frosted/grey-mauve lips surged while quick, tutorial-y Reels vastly outperformed static glam. Indie sparkle (multichromes, chrome highlighters, blue mascara) converted curiosity into clear purchase intent.

## 1. Overview

- The Vibe:
  - “Spooky spring” pastels meet alt-glam. Feeds mixed launch swatches (Shine By SD Audacious; Gourmande Girls Apparition; Unearthly winter bundles) with hands-on how-tos (halo eye, cream blush longevity, finger-blended base) and 20–60s “add-one-thing” transformations (glitter liner, blue mascara).
  - Heavy shimmer textures: chrome highlighters (ColourPop’s new formula), multichrome toppers (Clionadh, Terra Moons, Dieverune), and chunky glitter placed on lids and under-eyes.
- Engagement Temperature:
  - Warm-to-hot. Reels with a fast payoff and clear utility spiked (blue mascara wear test, 20-second glitter-liner “fairy” look). Followers asked specific shade/tech details, requested swatches, and signaled buys; some called for re-releases (Kaleidos OG Mars Melter) and packaging tweaks.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

- Top Mentioned Brands:
  - Indie sparkle core: Glisten Cosmetics, Unearthly Cosmetics, Terra Moons, Shine By SD, Gourmande Girls, Clionadh, Dieverune, Cosmic Brushes, Kaleidos, Touch of Glam, Fantasy Cosmetica, Dramo.
  - Complementary/mainstream: MAC, ColourPop, Essence, Urban Decay, NYX, L’Oréal, KJH.brand, Half Magic, Huda Beauty, Maybelline, Chanel, Kayali.

- Viral Products:

  - ColourPop chrome highlighters (Little Mermaid collab): high intrigue; side-by-side shade interest; calls to compare to Wet n Wild chameleons.
  - Glisten Spectra liners/stamps + Stella/Chroma gloss and Juicy Tints (Coconut): instant payoff “whimsy” looks; “need this” comments.
  - Shine By SD Audacious singles (Untamed Aura, Edge of Glow): swatch carousels drove wishlist adds; creators wore pre-launch.
  - Terra Moons x Ellie Starr singles (“Under Violet Skies,” “Zodiacal Light”): repeated usage; questions on textures.
  - Unearthly: Menacing Metal lipstick “Fractured Halo” (“I need this”), Woeful Whimsy palette used as blush (calls for bigger blush pan), winter bundles (EU retailer availability via Monolith boosted access).
  - Half Magic Glitterpuck “Princess Ghost”: blue-silver sparkle accent with Blend Bunny blues; demand for more shades persists.
  - KJH.brand: Prism Light frost lip crayons + “Carve” stylo as liner—soft, wearable frost system; cross-featured in lip combos.
  - CoverGirl “Bestie Boo” blue used as mixer with MAC Bare Maximal—created grungy grey-purple; comments confirmed immediate trial/purchase.

- The “Look” Breakdown:
  - Eyes:
    - Dominant: blue/teal/navy stories; chartreuse pops; icy silvers; multichrome toppers with wet foil reflect.
    - Techniques: mega-elongated graphic wings (navy/indigo), halo eyes (step-by-step static tutorial), heavy under-eye glitter drape/“teardrop” shapes.
    - Lashes: colored mascara (blue, navy) featured as the single quickest upgrade.
  - Lips:
    - Frost revival (drugstore L’Oréal Mica/Silverstone/Bronzine; KJH Prism Light), grey/greige mixers (blue+brown), deep cool browns and black-cherry reds (Necromancy), blue lips resurfacing in comments.
    - Multichrome/glitter gloss toppers (Unearthly, Dieverune) for ethereal sheen; “bitten” stains (Glisten Juicy Tints).
  - Cheeks:
    - Cool/novel tones: lavender blush/contour (custom Joocyee), green/blue chromatic highlighting (Kaleidos legacy; Cosmic Brushes Astral Radiance).
    - Black blush/“blushtour” (SushyGlow) used softly as contour, eye deepen-er, and to build ombré lips; broad curiosity and high views.
    - Cream blush longevity without setting (technique-forward; spray > light set > build).

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

- Stocking Recommendations:
  - Color mascara (blue/navy) at multiple price tiers; quick-payoff glitter liners (UD Moondust-type) and safe sparkle toppers (wet-effect multichromes without white base—Clionadh “glowy chrome” style).
  - Chrome highlighters (affordable + indie) in blue/green/pink shifts; blue-leaning and lavender highlighters.
  - Frost-forward lip wardrobe: wearable frosts (neutral-friendly), cool brown/black-cherry bullets, grey/blue mixers (liners, stain-balms), multichrome gloss toppers.
  - Novel cheek: lavender/grey-mauve blush/contour; “black blush” category (sheer, buildable); blue/green highlight trios.
  - Indie sparkle singles: Terra Moons, Shine By SD, Dieverune; include glitter adhesive/primer and “glimmer grasp”-type bases.
- Content Strategy:
  - Short-form, single-step upgrades (20–60s): colored mascara tests, glitter-liner demos, “mix this with that” lip alchemy.
  - Static, step-by-step graphics for eye techniques (halo, wing mapping) performed strongly for education-first audiences.
  - Swatch carousels in multiple lighting; arm-to-eye transitions; include skin-tone range swatches—audiences asked for brown-skin views.
  - Comparison clips (chrome highlight vs drugstore dupes; blue mascaras head-to-head) to capture “which one should I buy?” intent.
- Gap Analysis:
  - Packaging pain points: bulky compacts dampen purchase for collectors—curate sleek, stackable formats.
  - AI-designed packaging backlash among indie followers—lean into artist-collab/hand-designed aesthetics.
  - Glasses wear + base disruption: bundle nose-pad protectors/transfer-proof base kits; produce content on prevention.
  - Cream blush myths/confusion: sell by-finish (dewy vs set) and pair with technique content.

## 6. Special insights

- Emerging Micro-Trends:
  - Blue lips and navy liners/mascaras as normalized accents beyond editorial; “BLUE LIPS ARE SO BACK” appears in comments.
  - Frosted lips revival at drugstore and pro levels (L’Oréal frosts; KJH Prism Light) in cool nudes and metals.
  - Pastel-goth “spooky spring” palettes (Gourmande Girls Apparition; Glisten Bliss) combining soft mattes with high-voltage shimmers.
  - Lavender/grey-mauve “blushtour” and true black blush used softly for sculpt.
- Unexpected Pairings:
  - Blue lipstick as mixer to neutralize/or cool down reds/browns; navy liner + taupe speckled brows; eyeshadow pans used as blush (neon/green/purple), and black blush doubling as shadow and ombré lip shader.
  - One-minute “fairy” with glitter liner + blue mascara reframes sparkle as daytime-viable.
- Creative insight:
  - “Glowy chrome, no base” is the sweet spot: audiences loved iridescent shifts that work on deeper skins without whitening—curate a “sheer-shift” topper bar grouped by shift (UV violet, lime-to-cyan, pink-to-green).
  - Bundle “Vampy Curation” kits (cool reds, black balm, deep liners, burgundy shimmers) inspired by creator-curated beauty boxes; pair with a Pastel-Goth spring kit (mint/lilac mattes + chartreuse and icy-chrome toppers).
  - Lean into tools: small natural-fiber blush brushes that fit 23mm pans (noted as a need) and glitter-safe removers to reduce fallout anxiety.
