# Weekly Instagram Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key Takeaway: Apr 27–May 3, 2026 leaned hard into cool-toned sparkle and alt-glam: blue/purple multichrome lids, holographic highlighters used across the face, and colored lower-lash mascaras. Tutorial-style reels and swatch education around Half Magic x Euphoria, Clionadh, and Glisten outperformed static photos and drove clear purchase intent.

## 1. Overview

- The Vibe:
  - Heavy on chrome/duochrome swatches, formula explainers, and quick tutorials; fewer flatlays.
  - Alt-beauty aesthetics (goth/grunge) blended with “glass-sparkle” complexion—creams set to blur, then topped with iridescent/high-shine veils.
  - Event energy: creators traveling/speaking (The Makeup Show) boosted brand mentions (Glisten) and product demos.

- Engagement Temperature:
  - Warm-to-hot. Frequent “ordered/need” comments, code usage, and retailer questions (Ulta/EU availability).
  - Active curiosity on textures, creasing, depotting, and shade dupes; minor skepticism toward mainstream “36h no transfer” claims and shade ranges.

## 2. Product Momentum (Brand & Item Velocity)

- Top Mentioned Brands:
  - Half Magic (Euphoria palette + Glitter Puck), Glisten Cosmetics (Spectra Lash/liners/juicy tints), Clionadh, Dieverune, ColourPop, Nomad Cosmetics, What’s Up Beauty, Bella Beauté Bar (x Freya), Kiko Milano, Nabla, Melt, NYX, Haus Labs, TFIT.

  - CHEEKS:
    - Chrome shadows repurposed as highlighters (blue-violet/green shifts); “highlighter is back” sentiment.
    - Cream-to-powder, blurring blushes; muted neutrals/greige-brown blush (Red Chamber Nanmu) gaining traction.
  - LIPS:
    - Gray-pink “cement rose” combos (Haus Labs Light Peony + grey liners); vampy taupes/browns; olive/green moments; gradient glossy stains (NYX Lip IV).

## 5. Opportunities for the Store

- Stocking Recommendations:
  - Multichrome/iridescent toppers across price tiers:
    - Premium: Clionadh Stained Glass, Nabla Magichrome quad, Dieverune flakies/shimmers, Bella Beauté Bar holo shimmers.
    - Mid/Access: Half Magic singles/Glitter Puck analogs; Terra Moons blue–purple (Doppler Blue/Revati equivalents).
  - Colored mascaras: Broad shade range akin to Glisten Spectra Lash; prioritize light pastels (pink/cotton-candy/sky), royal blue, teal; ensure non-smudge, non-flake claims.
  - Stamp/graphic accents: Star/heart stamp pens; speckle liners; cobalt gel/pen liners.
  - Lips:
    - Grey-pink liners and pale taupe/brown mattes (Haus Labs Le Monster shades; greige lip pencils).
    - High-shine stains (NYX Lip IV-type) for summer.
  - Complexion add-ons: Cream-to-powder blurring blushes in muted greige/gray-brown; chrome highlighters with blue/green shifts.

- Content Strategy:
  - Short reels with:
    - Two-light setups (studio vs low light) to showcase shifts.
    - Side-by-side shade comparisons and texture callouts.
    - Multi-use demos (eye chrome as cheek/lip highlight).
    - “Lower-lash mascara color switch” quick tips; stamp accents.
  - Education sells: eye primer tests, cushion techniques, “don’t set your base” experiments; respond to common texture/creasing Qs.

- Gap Analysis:
  - Highlighter Impact: Some crave “blinding”—offer stronger reflect options alongside wet-sheen creams.
  - Distribution: Provide EU/US retailer guidance for Half Magic-like products; highlight store availability.
  - Mascara UX: Address feedback on drying and short bristles—stock options with longer comb wands and airtight packaging; offer minis.
  - Complexion Inclusivity: Note concerns around K-beauty/K-indie concealer shade gaps—curate inclusive ranges and clear undertone guides.
  - Setting Sprays: Skepticism about extreme no-transfer claims; position flexible, non-drying options (Skindinavia/bridal-type) and usage as primer vs set.

## 6. Special insights

- Emerging Micro-Trends:
  - Colored lower lashes as the only color on the eye (pastel pink/soybean/sky blue) for “minimal effort, max impact.”
  - Grey-brown/greige blush (contour-adjacent) on cool/olive undertones (Red Chamber Nanmu) as a new neutral.
  - Holos in grayscale palettes (Bella Beauté Bar x Freya) pushing “glam greyscale.”

- Unexpected Pairings:
  - Chrome shadows as face highlighters and lip toppers (Half Magic Rue, Dieverune All Hallow’s Eve).
  - Pro face liners (Kryolan) used as lip liners to desaturate pinks; blue balms to cool lip tones (MOB x Courtney).
  - Holo/flake textures placed off-lashline (outer-eye speckle) for editorial dimension.

- Creative insight:
  - The “sparkle mapping” era: artists deliberately place iridescent particles across planes (brow bone, cheek peak, bridge, Cupid’s bow) to read as skin texture in motion, not just “glitter.” Merchandising curated “sparkle kits” (chrome single + holo topper + precise brush + two-light demo) could capitalize on this behavior.
  - Event-driven affinity: Live appearances (The Makeup Show) correlate with surge in mentions and code use—mirror this by hosting timed IG Live swatch parties aligned to launches.
