# Weekly Top 10 Indie Makeup Product Mentions

**Reporting Period:** January 19–25, 2026

1.  **Succulent Dew Palette by Singe Beauty** — 13 mentions  
    This newly launched palette was the most discussed product, heavily featured in PR unboxings and tutorials. Creators praised its vibrant, cool-toned jewel story of teals, purples, and pinks, noting the blendable mattes and highly sparkly, smooth shimmers. The palette was used to create complex, pigmented eye looks and was highlighted for its cohesive color story and impressive $48 price point.

2.  **Bleeding Hearts Palette by Gourmande Girls** — 10 mentions  
    Creators lauded this palette for its unique take on a Valentine's Day theme, combining traditional pinks and reds with unexpected smoky and grungy tones like greys and deep greens. The formula received positive feedback for its high pigmentation in both mattes and impactful shimmers. The palette was used to create intense smoky eye looks, with its unique "pomegranate flower" red shimmer being a particular standout.

3.  **Evocative Blushes by Singe Beauty** — 9 mentions  
    The launch of four new shades, including cool-toned purples like "Fearless Fig," drove conversation around this blush formula. Creators consistently praised its glowy, skin-like finish that provides a luminous sheen without being glittery. The powder formula was noted for layering beautifully over liquid products and was highlighted by one creator as being exceptionally flattering on dry skin.

4.  **Nascent Palette by Starborn Cosmetics** — 9 mentions  
    As the debut launch from creator Milly (@badtothebrow), this palette generated significant anticipation and positive first impressions. It was consistently described as having a "grungy" and "earthy" color story with buildable mattes and sparkly, multi-dimensional shimmers. Several creators appreciated the "dry texture" of the shimmers, noting they performed well on hooded eyes without creasing.

5.  **Pinky Promise Palette by Bella Beaute Bar** — 7 mentions  
    This collaboration with creator Deandra Nicole was celebrated as the "ultimate pink palette," featuring a vibrant, monochromatic range of pinks and berries. It was specifically praised for its excellent depth and for being designed to work well on tan, deep, and dark skin tones. Creators demonstrated its high pigmentation and blendability through smooth, saturated swatches and complex gradient eye looks.

6.  **Heart to Get Collection by ColourPop** — 6 mentions  
    This Valentine's collection garnered attention for its cool-toned approach and 90s-inspired heart-shaped packaging. The Super Shock Blushes were the clear standout, with creators loving their intense, glowing finish. While opinions on the eyeshadow palette were mixed, the overall collection was discussed positively for its aesthetic and the performance of its cheek and lip products.

7.  **Veil of Winter Skies Collection by Terra Moons Cosmetics** — 5 mentions  
    This winter-themed collection was praised for its moody, desaturated cool-toned mattes paired with icy, bright shimmers. Creators highlighted the brand's first holographic shades for their smooth texture and intense effect. The highly sparkly, diamond-like topper shade "Ice Flare" and the electric blue iridescent "Polar Ion" were frequently mentioned as favorites.

8.  **Edward Scissorhands Collection by Melt Cosmetics** — 3 mentions  
    This collection was featured by a creator who purchased it and declared the lip liner and blush to be new favorites. The brand's pencil eyeliners were also praised as being the "best." The products were used to create moody makeup looks that aligned with the collection's gothic theme.

9.  **Twilight Eclipse Collection by ColourPop** — 3 mentions  
    Following the success of the brand's first Twilight collaboration, this collection was met with excitement. Creators were intrigued by the moody, cool-toned color story featuring neutrals and a deep matte red, a shade they noted ColourPop executes well. High expectations were set based on the excellent quality of the previous palette in the series.

10. **Paris La Bohème Palette by Nomad Cosmetics** — 2 mentions  
    This palette was praised for its unique dual color story, which combines dusty pinks and purples with swampy greens and blues. In demonstrations, the matte shades appeared highly pigmented and blended smoothly. The shimmers were noted to be intensely bright with a strong duochrome color shift, impressing creators with their performance.
