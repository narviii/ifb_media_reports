# Weekly YouTube Beauty Media Landscape Report

- Key takeaway: Indie palettes with moody, seasonal color stories dominated, while creators converged on “all‑matte + topper” eye strategies, blush layering, and comfortable-but-impactful lips. Texture scrutiny (emollient shimmers vs drier metallics) is now a primary purchase filter.

## 1. Overview

This week felt like peak indie seasonality: goth/alt aesthetics, dark romance cherries, and moody “Pacific Northwest” nature stories. Creators leaned into artistry and pigment-first discussion over brand hype, with affiliate links used but balanced by real critique.

There’s visible fatigue with generic holiday vaults and repackaged shades; enthusiasm concentrates where formulas or curation clearly advance (e.g., Danessa adding mattes to Lightwork, Blend Bunny’s comprehensive matte range, Nomad’s cohesive muted blues/greens). Value talk bifurcates: collectors justify premium multichromes if performance and versatility are proven, while viral extreme-value drops (e.l.f. Big Gripper) win on cost-per-use.

Texture talk was constant. Emollient shimmers that crease without a tacky base drew warnings; drier, high-shine metallics and toppers earned praise for longevity and control. Many looks explicitly used glitter primer, signaling a norm for specialty shimmer deployment.

Complexion saw a seasonal pivot: stacked blush (cream + powder) in terracotta/berry/raisin with draping placement to sculpt. Lips skewed two ways—sparkly balm/glaze hybrids (high comfort, visible shift) and vampy, liner-led combos layered with gloss for a modern, non-matte finish.

## 3. Category Trends

- Eyes: Momentum in all‑matte palettes plus topper/iridescent companions; moody greens/blues, cherries/berries, and grunge/“mold” tones. Glitter/tacky primers are treated as standard tools with indie shimmers.
- Highlighter: Ethereal purple-blue shifts and wet-look sticks gain visibility (Dark Dreamscape, R.E.M. glow stick).
- Lips: Sparkly balm/glaze hybrids (comfort + visible shift) and deep vamp liners with glossy overlays; comfortable hyper-matte liquids from indie brands remain niche favorites.
- Cheeks: Layered blush (cream under powder) and draping placement; shades trend terracotta, raisin, burnt rose for fall cohesion.
- Tools/primers: Grip primers (viral value) and glitter primers as essential adjuncts to specialty pigments.
- Seasonal shift: Strong “spooky/goth” and dark nature aesthetics dominate October; carryover likely through early holiday due to jewel-tone stories.

## 4. Emerging Themes & Narratives

- All‑matte + topper architecture: Multiple creators endorse an all‑matte base (Blend Bunny) paired with topper/iridescent shimmer add‑ons for control, depth, and longevity.
- Texture discipline: Cross‑creator alignment that emollient shimmers need tacky bases and may crease; drier metallics earn preference for wear and precision.
- Dark cherry/dark feminine revival: Black Cherry becomes a seasonal anchor across channels, pushing berry/burgundy back into rotation with modern shimmer rows.
- Goth/alt seasonal surge: Nevermore, Rotten, Conjuring tie-ins, and Hela restocks validate sustained appetite for dark, conceptual stories beyond Halloween week.
- Value polarization: High-end multichrome sets justify with added mattes/customizability (Lightwork VII) while drugstore wins mindshare with viral extreme-value plays (e.l.f. jug).
- Collaboration culture with quality filter: Creator/influencer collabs (Blend Bunny x Mei Pang; Fantasy Cosmetica x Battybean) receive trust; generic IP collabs get scrutinized unless “deep lore” packaging is executed thoughtfully.
- Regional/brand-origin signals: Western indie (US/AU/UK) leads visibility; Oden’s Eye (Sweden) sustains restock demand; increased US distribution (Glaminatrix via Monolith) reduces friction and boosts US purchase likelihood; indie at mass retail (Sigma at Ulta) broadens discovery.

## 5. Purchase-Intent Signals

- Scarcity/return drops convert: Oden’s Eye Hela restock and limited collabs (Rotten, Witch’s Familiar) trigger immediate buys.
- Functionality upgrades justify premium: Danessa adding mattes to Lightwork addresses past criticism, unlocking “worth it” sentiment despite $128.
- System selling: Matte workhorse + topper bundle logic, and blush “combo” recipes (cream + powder) push multi-item baskets.
- Comfort + effect on lips: Bella Beauté Bar Lip Sparkle Balms and Unearthly hyper-matte liquids generate repeat enthusiasm due to comfort, non-gritty sparkle, and shift.
- Theme-first curation: PNW earthy moody and dark cherry stories show high resonance; shoppers want cohesive, story-driven edits over generic big kits.
- Enablers convert: Glitter primer, grip primers, and liner pencils frequently credited for best results—ripe for attachment sales.

## 6. Opportunities for the Store

- Stock/feature high-momentum SKUs (or analogous aesthetics)
  - Eyeshadow: Simply Posh Black Cherry; Nomad Pacific Northwest; Blend Bunny x Mei Pang Rotten + Efflorescence; DeDe Signature From The Roots; Gourmande Girls Nevermore. If direct access is limited, curate equivalents: all‑matte deep undertone palette + iridescent topper set; moody green/blue jewel tones; burgundy/berry rows with drier metallics.
  - Lips: Bella Beauté Bar Lip Sparkle Balms (or comparable non‑gritty sparkly balm/glaze); Unearthly hyper‑matte liquids; deep vamp liners.
  - Highlighters: Purple/blue-shift highlighters with translucent bases to meet the “ethereal” demand.

- Build “systems” and bundles
  - All‑Matte Artist Kit: comprehensive matte palette + topper quad + black/brown/vamp liners.
  - Shimmer Success Kit: glitter/tacky primer + drier metallic singles + sealing medium; include application tips card.
  - Fall Cheek Duos: pre‑paired cream + powder blush in terracotta/raisin/burnt rose with draping guide.

- Prioritize textures creators prefer
  - Source drier, high-reflect metallics to counter emollient-creasing complaints; keep select emollient flakies but position them with mandatory base recommendations.

- Attach the enablers
  - Always cross-sell glitter primer, grip primer, and detail brushes with multichrome orders; add small discounts for add‑ons at checkout.

- Lean into seasonal editorial
  - Curate “Dark Cherry,” “Rotten Greens,” and “PNW Blues & Moss” landing pages with full-face looks and product maps mirroring creator aesthetics.

- Avoid/limit
  - Oversized, all‑in‑one cream complexion palettes and generic holiday vaults (consistent creator rejection).
  - Low-lore, overused IP collabs unless packaging/details are exceptional.

- Distribution and access wins
  - Where possible, partner with indie brands improving US/EU logistics (e.g., AU brands shipping from US) to reduce delivery friction; highlight “ships from [region]” badges.

- Content and education
  - Publish quick guides: “Stop Shimmer Creasing” (why tacky bases matter), “All‑Matte to Multichrome in 3 Steps,” and “Blush Draping 101” to convert browsing into confident carts.

Overall, stock into the matte-plus-topper eye workflow, blush layering duos, and comfortable sparkly lips; frame them within dark seasonal stories and back them with technique education to capture current intent and raise basket sizes.
